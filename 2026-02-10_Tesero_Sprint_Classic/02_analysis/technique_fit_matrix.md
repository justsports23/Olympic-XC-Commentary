## Key Insights

### Athletes whose profiles MATCH this course:
1. **Klæbo** — Perfect fit. Literally designed for this course.
2. **Anger** — His 1.90m frame, double-poling power, and endurance match the long, climbing course.
3. **Mocellini** — 2nd-fastest qualifier proves his time-trial fitness matches the course profile.
4. **Myhlback** — Vasaloppet endurance is uniquely valuable on this long, climbing sprint course.
5. **Schumacher** — Self-described "capacity" advantage; distance specialist on a distance-like sprint course.

### Athletes whose profiles DON'T match this course:
1. **Chanavat** — Classic technique weakness is devastating. 27th-44th in classic qualifications. May not make top 30.
2. **Vuorinen** — Freestyle specialist in a classic event. Championship bronze was in freestyle.
3. **Pellegrino** — Classic sprint form declining at 35. Qualified 25th on this course in January. Relies heavily on tactics and championship magic.

### The "Endurance Sprint" Effect
This course's length (1,585m) and climbing (49m) create what could be called an "endurance sprint" — a format that rewards:
- Athletes with high aerobic capacity (Myhlback, Schumacher, Anger)
- Athletes with strong classic climbing technique (Klæbo, Chappaz, Grahn)

And penalizes:
- Pure flat-track speed specialists (Chanavat)
- Freestyle sprint specialists (Vuorinen, Grond)
- Athletes who rely on final-50m kicks rather than sustained effort
