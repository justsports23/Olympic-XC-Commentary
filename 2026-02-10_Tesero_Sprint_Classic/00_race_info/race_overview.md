## Format Details

### Qualification Round
- All entered athletes ski the 1,585m course individually against the clock
- Athletes start at 15-second intervals
- The **30 fastest** qualifiers advance to the knockout rounds
- All remaining athletes are eliminated

### Quarterfinals (5 heats × 6 athletes)
- 30 qualified athletes are drawn into 5 heats of 6 skiers
- Top 2 finishers in each heat advance automatically (10 athletes)
- 2 additional "lucky losers" (fastest non-qualifying times across all heats) also advance
- **12 athletes** advance to semifinals

### Semifinals (2 heats × 6 athletes)
- 12 athletes drawn into 2 heats of 6 skiers
- Top 2 finishers in each heat advance automatically (4 athletes)
- 2 additional "lucky losers" also advance
- **6 athletes** advance to the Final

### Final
- 6 athletes race head-to-head
- First to cross the finish line wins **Olympic Gold**
- Finishing positions determine Gold, Silver, and Bronze
- Times do not carry over from any previous round

## Venue Context

The Cross Country and Biathlon Center Fabio Canal in Tesero is located in the Val di Fiemme valley in Trentino, northeastern Italy. It sits at approximately **1,000m elevation**. The venue has hosted multiple FIS Cross-Country World Cup events, Tour de Ski stages, and the 1991, 2003, and 2013 FIS Nordic World Ski Championships.

For the 2026 Olympics, the venue was upgraded to accommodate both cross-country skiing and biathlon events. The stadium area features a purpose-built finish straight and grandstands.
