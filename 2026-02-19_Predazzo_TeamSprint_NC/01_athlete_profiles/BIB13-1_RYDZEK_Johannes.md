## Career Highlights
- **Olympic results:** Vancouver 2010 — Bronze (Team); Sochi 2014 — Silver (Team LH/4x5km); PyeongChang 2018 — **Gold** (Individual LH), **Gold** (Team LH/4x5km); Beijing 2022 — competed (no medals); Milano Cortina 2026 — competing in Team Sprint
- **World Championship results:** 8 participations; **14 medals total** — 7 x Gold, 6 x Silver, 1 x Bronze; HISTORIC 2017 Lahti grand slam: won ALL FOUR gold medals (Individual NH, Individual LH, Team NH, Team Sprint) — unprecedented [Axelent](https://www.axelent.de/x-news-old/2017/historical-performance-at-the-world-championships-in-lahti-by-johannes-rydzek/); 2025 Trondheim: Team LH Gold (Germany's first team gold since 2017)
- **World Cup wins/podiums:** 69 podiums — 26 x 1st, 28 x 2nd, 15 x 3rd; first WC win March 2011 Lahti
- **Other major results:**
  - Holmenkollen Medal recipient (2021) — most prestigious award in Nordic skiing [FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/nordic-combined/news/2021-22/holmenkollen-medal-for-johannes-rydzek)
  - German Male Athlete of the Year (2017)
  - Ended 6-year (2,150 days) WC win drought with Ruka victory in November 2024 [FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/nordic-combined/news/2024-25/rydzek-ends-six-year-wait-for-wins-with-dream-return-in-ruka)

## Key Storylines
- **One of the most decorated Nordic Combined athletes in history** — 14 World Championship medals, 4 Olympic medals, Holmenkollen Medal, 69 WC podiums
- Five-time Olympian at age 34 — remarkable longevity at the highest level
- Historic 2017 Lahti grand slam remains unmatched in the sport's history
- Emotional comeback: ended 6-year World Cup win drought at Ruka in November 2024
- Partnered with Vinzenz Geiger — Germany arguably has the second-strongest team behind Norway
- From Oberstdorf, Bavaria — same club as Geiger
- This could be the final Olympic NC event ever — Rydzek would be a fitting champion for the sport's farewell
- Equipment sponsor: Viessmann, Axelent, Uvex
