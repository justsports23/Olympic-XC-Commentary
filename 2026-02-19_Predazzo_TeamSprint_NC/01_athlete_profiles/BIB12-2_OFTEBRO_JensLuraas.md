## Career Highlights
- **Olympic results:** Beijing 2022 — **Gold** (Team LH/4x5km); Milano Cortina 2026 — **3 x GOLD** (Individual NH/10km, Individual LH/10km, Team event) and **1 x Silver**
- **World Championship results:** 3 participations; 4 x Gold (including Mixed Team 2025 Trondheim with Hansen/Riiber/Hagen; Team 2021 Oberstdorf); 2 x Silver (including Individual LH 2023 Planica, Compact 2025); 2 x Bronze (including Sprint 2021)
- **World Cup wins/podiums:** 46 podiums — 17 x 1st, 12 x 2nd, 17 x 3rd; 2024-2025 Overall World Cup Champion
- **Other major results:**
  - First World Cup podium: 3rd at Ruka, November 2019 (age 19)
  - First World Cup victory: Ruka, November 2020
  - Holds all three Nordic Combined Olympic titles from Milano Cortina
  - Brother Einar Luraas Oftebro also competes — maiden WC win at Otepää 2025-2026

## Technique & Format Profile
- **Ski Jumping strength:** Good but not dominant — typically finishes mid-pack in jumping phase
- **Cross-Country strength:** **ELITE — the best cross-country skier in Nordic Combined today**; overcame 22-second deficit to win Olympic LH gold; "Never spectacular in jumping, but formidable in cross-country skiing" [Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/milano-cortina-2026/results/athlete-details/42330)
- **Sprint ability:** Strong — Team Sprint gold medalist, relay format specialist
- **Distance preference:** All formats — equally dominant in Gundersen, mass start, compact
- **Best format:** Individual Gundersen (multiple Olympic and WC wins)
- **Climbing ability:** Elite — can make up massive time gaps on demanding courses
