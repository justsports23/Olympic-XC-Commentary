## Career Highlights
- **Olympic results:** Sochi 2014 (debut); PyeongChang 2018; Beijing 2022; Milano Cortina 2026 — **BRONZE** (Individual Gundersen LH/10km, time 24:59.8, +14.8s from gold)
- **World Championship results:** 7 participations; **Silver** in Individual Gundersen NH/10km at 2021 Oberstdorf
- **World Cup wins/podiums:** 21 podiums — 2 x 1st, 7 x 2nd, 12 x 3rd
  - **Maiden individual WC victory:** Oslo/Holmenkollen March 2025 — Individual Gundersen Compact — hill record jump of 146.0m; won by 34 seconds; first Finnish individual WC win in 15 years (since Hannu Manninen 2009-10) [FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/nordic-combined/news/2024-25/herola-storms-to-maiden-win-as-riiber-bows-out-and-geiger-moves-to-brink-of-crystal-globe)
  - **Team Sprint WC victory:** Lahti 2019 (with Eero Hirvonen, by 0.2 seconds over Norway)
- **Other major results:**
  - Holmenkollen hill record (146.0m) during Oslo victory
  - Broke 15-year Finnish WC win drought

## Technique & Format Profile
- **Ski Jumping strength:** Strong — Holmenkollen hill record 146.0m; capable of leading after SJ phase
- **Cross-Country strength:** ELITE — Olympic bronze came from 7th after jump to briefly leading the race before finishing 3rd; dominant on skis
- **Sprint ability:** Team Sprint World Cup winner with Hirvonen — proven relay format specialist
- **Distance preference:** Standard Gundersen (10km), compact (7.5km), and team sprint
- **Best format:** All formats — versatile competitor; Team Sprint winner with same partner racing today
- **Climbing ability:** Strong — excels on demanding XC courses

## Key Storylines
- **Olympic bronze medalist at LH/10km** — started 7th after jumping, briefly led the race, finished 3rd behind Oftebro and Lamparter
- Quote: "It's been a long time without the medal and, of course, after all these years, it feels great to finally achieve it"
- **Four-time Olympian** at age 30 — career-defining Olympic medal after 12 years on the circuit
- **Maiden WC victory breakthrough:** Won first individual World Cup at Oslo in March 2025 after 13 seasons — broke 15-year Finnish drought
- **Proven Team Sprint partner with Hirvonen** — they won the WC Team Sprint at Lahti 2019 by just 0.2 seconds
- Both Finnish athletes (Hirvonen + Herola) won Olympic bronze medals this week — team riding a wave of confidence
- Passionate musician — plays drums in a Nordic Combined athlete band with Bernhard Gruber (AUT) and Tino Edelmann (GER)
- Lives in Kuopio/Siilinjarvi, Finland; competes for Puijon Hiihtoseura
- IOC Athletes' Commission candidate at Milano Cortina 2026 [Finnish Olympic Committee](https://www.olympiakomitea.fi/en/current/news/nordic-combined-athlete-ilkka-herola-candidate-to-the-iocs-athletes-commission/)
