## Career Highlights
- **Olympic results:** Beijing 2022 — 4th (two events, just missed podium); Milano Cortina 2026 — **Silver** (Individual NH/10km, +1.0s behind Oftebro), **Silver** (Individual LH/10km, +5.9s behind Oftebro)
- **World Championship results:** 3 participations; 2 x Gold (including Individual LH Gundersen 2021, Team Sprint 2021); 1 x Silver; 5 x Bronze (multiple team bronzes at 2023 Planica and 2025 Trondheim)
- **World Cup wins/podiums:** 62 podiums — 22 x 1st, 29 x 2nd, 11 x 3rd; 2022-23 Crystal Globe winner; currently leading 2025-26 standings with 5+ wins this season
- **Other major results:**
  - 2022-23 Overall WC Champion — first Austrian Crystal Globe since Gottwald 2000-01
  - 5 WC wins in 2025-26 season alone (Ruka Tour, Trondheim, Seefeld, Otepää, plus more)
  - Red Bull sponsored athlete

## Technique & Format Profile
- **Ski Jumping strength:** Very strong — consistent top-level jumper; can build leads in SJ
- **Cross-Country strength:** Elite — capable of winning from any position; strong in tactical racing
- **Sprint ability:** World Championship Team Sprint gold medalist (2021) — proven in this format
- **Distance preference:** All formats — equally dominant across all NC formats
- **Best format:** All — Crystal Globe winner, versatile champion
- **Climbing ability:** Elite — consistent medal contender on all courses

## Key Storylines
- **Double Olympic silver medalist** — lost both events to Oftebro by 1.0s and 5.9s respectively; the dominant rivalry of these Games
- **Currently leading the World Cup overall** with 5 wins this season — the strongest form of his career
- At 24, is the youngest of the elite contenders — has decades of career ahead (though NC leaves Olympics)
- Partners with Stefan Rettenegger (16 WC podiums, 0 wins) — Austria has enormous combined strength
- Coached by Christoph Eugen; trained by Andi Felder since age 6 at Nordic Team Absam
- Fischer skis, Leki poles
- If Norway slips in jumping, Austria is the prime beneficiary -- Lamparter can compete with Oftebro on the track
