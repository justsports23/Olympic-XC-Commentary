# Venue History — Predazzo / Val di Fiemme
## Nordic Combined Events

---

## Venue Overview

**Predazzo Ski Jumping Stadium (Trampolino Giuseppe Dal Ben)**
- Location: Predazzo, Trentino, Italy (Fiemme Valley)
- Inaugurated: 1989
- Large Hill: HS 104 (Olympic configuration); historically HS 134-143 for World Cup/Worlds
- Cross-Country: Tesero Cross-Country Skiing Stadium (adjacent town in Fiemme Valley)
- **21 Nordic Combined World Cup event weekends** hosted at this venue [NBC Olympics](https://nbcolympics.com/news/nordic-combined-101-venue)

---

## World Championships at Val di Fiemme

### 1991 FIS Nordic World Ski Championships
- First World Championships hosted at Val di Fiemme
- Included Nordic Combined events (full program)
- Established the venue's credentials on the world stage

### 2003 FIS Nordic World Ski Championships (Feb 18 – Mar 1, 2003)
- Full Nordic Combined program
- **Ski Jump record:** Adam Małysz (POL) set distance record of 136m [NBC Olympics](https://nbcolympics.com/news/nordic-combined-101-venue)
- Finland's Samppa Lajunen won bronze in team events [FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sectorcode=NK&competitorid=33576)

### 2013 FIS Nordic World Ski Championships (Feb 20 – Mar 3, 2013)
- **Individual Large Hill Gold:** Eric Frenzel (GER) — also set new ski jump record of 138.5m [FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/nordic-combined/news/2018-19/articleeric-frenzel)
- **Team Relay Gold (4x5km):** France (Braud, Laheurte, Lacroix, Lamy Chappuis); Norway silver; USA bronze [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FIS_Nordic_World_Ski_Championships_2013)
- **Historic debut of Team Sprint format** at World Championship level [Fiemme World Cup](https://www.fiemmeworldcup.com/en/organising-committee/fiemme-world-championships/Fiemme-2013)

## World Cup History at Val di Fiemme

### January 2017 World Cup
- **Team Sprint result:** Italy (Samuel Costa + partner) finished **3rd** — Costa's home-venue podium
- Costa's Team Sprint experience at this specific venue is highly relevant for the 2026 Olympic Team Sprint

### January 2019 World Cup (3-day event)
| Date | Event | Winner | Second | Third |
|------|-------|--------|--------|-------|
| Jan 11, 2019 | Individual Gundersen 10km | Johannes Rydzek (GER) 26:58.4 | Jørgen Gråbak (NOR) +5.9s | [DATA NOT FOUND] |
| Jan 12, 2019 | **Team Sprint 2x7.5km** | **Norway** (Schmid/Gråbak) 34:30.9 | **Germany I** (Rydzek/Geiger) +0.5s | **Germany II** (Frenzel/Rießle) +0.5s |
| Jan 13, 2019 | Individual Gundersen 10km | **Vinzenz Geiger (GER)** 26:34.0 (**maiden WC win**) | Johannes Rydzek (GER) | [DATA NOT FOUND] |

[Inside the Games](https://www.insidethegames.biz/articles/1074178/geiger-claims-maiden-nordic-combined-world-cup-win-in-val-di-fiemme)

**Key connections to 2026 Olympic Team Sprint field:**
- **Geiger (BIB 13-2)** scored his FIRST WC win here — Val di Fiemme is his breakthrough venue
- **Rydzek (BIB 13-1)** won the first day and was 2nd on day three — strong venue history
- **Norway won the Team Sprint** in 2019 (Schmid/Gråbak) — demonstrates Norway's Team Sprint strength at this venue
- **Germany was 2nd AND 3rd** in the 2019 Team Sprint — the Rydzek/Geiger pairing has specific Team Sprint experience at Predazzo

### 2019-2020 World Cup
- Jarl Magnus Riiber (NOR) won at Val di Fiemme in both 2019 and 2020 [Fiemme World Cup](https://www.fiemmeworldcup.com/en/nordiccombined/ne/458333/riiber-wins-the-first-gundersen-in-val-di-fiemme.php)

### January 2022 World Cup — Historic Firsts
- First-ever **women's Nordic Combined World Cup** event held at Val di Fiemme
- Gyda Westvold Hansen (NOR) claimed inaugural victory [The Sports](https://www.the-sports.org/nordic-combined-women-s-world-cup-val-di-fiemme-results-2021-2022-epr117719.html)
- Mixed Team event also debuted here

### 2024-25 Season
- Planned WC events for January 10-11, 2025 were **CANCELLED** due to delays in reconstruction of Trampolino Dal Ben (HS 143) [FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/event-details.html?sectorcode=NK&eventid=43137)
- This means athletes have not competed at Predazzo since 2022 — limited recent venue familiarity
