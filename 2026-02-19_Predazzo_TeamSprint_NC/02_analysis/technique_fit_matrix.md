# Technique Fit Matrix — Nordic Combined Team Sprint LH/2x7.5km
## Milano Cortina 2026 Olympics | Predazzo, 19 February 2026

---

## Race Demands Profile

This event requires teams to excel across TWO distinct disciplines:

### 1. Ski Jumping (Large Hill, HS 104)
- Predazzo's HS 104 with K-Point at 95m
- Requires: flight technique, takeoff timing, wind reading, nerve under pressure
- Each athlete gets ONE jump — no second chances
- Combined team score determines XC start gaps

### 2. Cross-Country Sprint (2x7.5km)
- Sprint relay format — alternating legs between two athletes
- Requires: explosive speed, fast recovery between legs, relay transition efficiency
- Multiple short high-intensity efforts rather than one sustained effort
- Tactical positioning and drafting in groups is critical

### Key Course Factors
- **Sprint intensity:** Shorter legs = higher average speed = rewards anaerobic power
- **Recovery ability:** Multiple legs with rest intervals = rewards athletes who recover quickly
- **Relay chemistry:** Tag exchanges, pacing strategy, and communication between teammates matter
- **Team balance:** A weak link costs time EVERY rotation — you can't hide one athlete

## Athlete-Specific Sprint Suitability

### Sprint Specialists (Explosive, High-Tempo Racers)
- **Oftebro (NOR):** Elite — sprinted to gold in both individual events with devastating closing speed
- **Lamparter (AUT):** Elite — 2021 WC Team Sprint champion, strong in all fast formats
- **Herola (FIN):** Strong — maiden WC victory included hill-record jump + tactical closing speed
- **Geiger (GER):** Elite when fit — Beijing 2022 was the ultimate sprint finish from behind

### Endurance Specialists (Better Over Longer Efforts)
- **Watabe (JPN):** 37 years old, better suited to sustained effort than repeated sprints — may fade in later legs
- **Rydzek (GER):** 34 years old, tactical intelligence compensates for any decline in raw sprint speed
- **Costa (ITA):** 33 years old, returned from retirement — endurance-based but sprint relay is demanding

### Recovery Questions (Ability to Repeat Efforts)
- **Teder (EST):** At 16-17, has youth on his side — but has never experienced this intensity at Olympic level
- **Hirvonen (FIN):** 30 years old — in the optimal zone for repeated high-intensity efforts
- **Skoglund (NOR):** 24 years old — youth and fitness should allow strong recovery between legs

**Key insight:** On paper, Austria has the best technique fit for this race — elite jumping AND strong XC from both athletes. But Norway's Oftebro factor warps the math. His XC ability is so far ahead of anyone else that it compensates for any jumping deficit. The real question is whether Skoglund can hold his own against Rettenegger and Lamparter on the legs where Oftebro isn't racing.

Finland's perfect Team Sprint format experience — the only pair who have won a Team Sprint together — is an underrated edge in a format that rewards relay chemistry as much as raw talent.
