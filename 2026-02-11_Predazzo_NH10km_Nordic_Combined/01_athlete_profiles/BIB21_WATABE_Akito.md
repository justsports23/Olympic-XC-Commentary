## Career Highlights
- **Olympic Results — 6TH OLYMPIC GAMES (record-tying):**
  - Torino 2006: 19th (Individual Sprint) — debuted at age 17 while in high school ([Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/athletes/akito-watabe))
  - Vancouver 2010: 21st (NH), 9th (LH) ([Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/athletes/akito-watabe))
  - Sochi 2014: **SILVER** (Individual NH/10km) — Japan's first Nordic skiing medal in 20 years ([Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/athletes/akito-watabe))
  - PyeongChang 2018: **SILVER** (Individual NH/10km) ([Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/athletes/akito-watabe))
  - Beijing 2022: **BRONZE** (Individual LH/10km) + **BRONZE** (Team) ([Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/news/watabe-akito-reax-two-more-nordic-combined-medals-beijing-2022))
  - Milano Cortina 2026: **6th Olympic appearance**
  - **Total Olympic medals: 2 Silver, 2 Bronze = 4 medals**
  - **First athlete ever to win 3 consecutive individual Olympic medals in Nordic Combined**
- **World Championship Results:**
  - 1 Gold (Team, 2009 Liberec), 1 Silver, 3 Bronze ([Wikipedia — Akito Watabe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe))
- **World Cup:** 19 individual victories (Japanese record); 2017-18 Overall Crystal Globe champion ([FIS Athlete Biography](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sectorcode=NK&competitorid=89882))
- **2017-18 Season:** 8 individual WC wins in one season (Japanese record)
- **Holmenkollen Medal:** Awarded 2025 — one of Nordic skiing's highest honors

## Key Storylines

### The Last Dance — Final Season
Akito Watabe has announced he will **retire at the end of the 2025-26 season**, making Milano Cortina 2026 his farewell Olympics. This is his **6th Olympic Games** — an extraordinary span from Torino 2006 (when he was a 17-year-old high school student) to Milano Cortina 2026 (at age 37). ([Nordic Mag](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/nordic-combined-japanese-legend-akito-watabe-to-retire-at-the-end-of-the-season-1023/))

### "I Know the Mountain Better Than Anyone"
After winning two medals at Beijing 2022, Watabe said: "I know the mountain better than anyone." His experience across 6 Olympics is unmatched by any athlete in the field. ([Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/news/watabe-akito-reax-two-more-nordic-combined-medals-beijing-2022))

### Inspired by the 1998 Olympics
Watabe grew up in Hakuba, Nagano — where the 1998 Winter Olympics were held. As a 10-year-old, he watched the ski jumpers at those Games and fell in love with the sport. Twenty-eight years later, he's still competing at the highest level. ([Waseda University](https://www.waseda.jp/inst/athletic/news-en/2022/03/09/25644/?lng=en))

### 300 World Cup Starts
At the Seefeld Triple in January 2026, Watabe made his **300th World Cup start** — a milestone of incredible longevity in any sport. ([Nordic Mag](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/nordic-combined-triple-in-seefeld-a-special-number-for-akito-watabes-300th-world-cup-start-0130/))

### Family Dynasty
- Married to **Yurie Watabe**, a Japanese freestyle skiing (halfpipe) Olympian ([Wikipedia — Akito Watabe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe))
- His brother **Yoshito Watabe** is also a Nordic Combined athlete
- In 2013, Akito and Yoshito became the first Japanese brothers on a World Cup podium together since the Ogiwara brothers in 1995

### The Sport He Defined in Japan
Watabe's 19 World Cup wins are a Japanese record. He brought unprecedented attention to Nordic Combined in Japan and was awarded the Holmenkollmedaljen in 2025 — the highest honor in Nordic skiing.

## Personal Life
- **Wife:** Yurie Watabe (née Yamazaki), Olympic freestyle skier (halfpipe). Married May 24, 2014. She competed at the 2014 Sochi Olympics in halfpipe skiing ([Wikipedia — Akito Watabe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe))
- **Son:** Born November 2020. After winning bronze at Beijing 2022, Watabe emotionally said he just wanted to go home to play with his son ([Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/news/watabe-akito-reax-two-more-nordic-combined-medals-beijing-2022))
- **Brother:** Yoshito Watabe, also a Nordic Combined World Cup competitor. In 2013, they became the first Japanese brothers on a World Cup podium together since the legendary Ogiwara brothers in 1995 ([Wikipedia — Akito Watabe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akito_Watabe))
- **Childhood:** Grew up in Hakuba, Nagano — site of the 1998 Winter Olympics. As a 10-year-old, he watched ski jumpers at those Games and fell in love with the sport ([Waseda University](https://www.waseda.jp/inst/athletic/news-en/2022/03/09/25644/?lng=en))
- **Education:** Attended Nagano Prefectural Hakuba High School, then Waseda University (Faculty of Sport Sciences) — one of Japan's most prestigious universities ([Waseda University](https://www.waseda.jp/inst/athletic/news-en/2022/03/09/25644/?lng=en))
- **Hobbies:** Mountain biking, reading widely. Known for seeking diverse perspectives and avoiding tunnel vision — described as unusually well-read for a professional athlete ([Goldwin](https://www.goldwin-global.com/us/feature/akito-watabe/))
- **Retirement:** Announced October 23, 2025 that the 2025-26 season would be his last ([Nordic Mag](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/nordic-combined-japanese-legend-akito-watabe-to-retire-at-the-end-of-the-season-1023/))
