## Career Highlights
- **2022 Winter Olympics (Beijing):** Competed in both individual events -- finished **32nd** in the Normal Hill/10km (22nd in jumping with 53.9 pts, XC 30:30.3) and **36th** in the Large Hill/10km (35th in jumping with 82.5 pts) ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dmytro_Mazurchuk), [Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/athletes/dmytro-mazurchuk))
- **2026 Winter Olympics (Milano Cortina):** Returns for his second Olympics alongside teammate Shumbarets, part of Ukraine's largest Winter Olympic delegation since 2010 ([Mezha.net](https://mezha.net/eng/bukvy/ukraine-announces-largest-winter-olympics-team-for-2026-milan-cortina-games/))
- **2025 FIS Nordic World Ski Championships:** 37th in Men's Gundersen 10km Large Hill -- his best World Championship result ([the-sports.org](https://www.the-sports.org/dmytro-mazurchuk-ski-jumping-spf358145.html))
- **2023 Winter World University Games (Lake Placid):** Bronze medalist in the Team Sprint event alongside Vitalii Hrebeniuk -- **Ukraine's first-ever medal in Nordic Combined at the Winter Universiade**. Also finished 5th in the Normal Hill/10km and 7th in the Mass Start 10km/Normal Hill ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dmytro_Mazurchuk))
- **Historic World Cup Result (2024-25):** Together with Shumbarets, achieved 37th and 40th at Ruka -- the first time two Ukrainian athletes scored in the same World Cup event. The first Ukrainian WC points since Mazurchuk's own 30th-place finish at Val di Fiemme on January 9, 2022 ([Ukrainska Pravda](https://www.pravda.com.ua/eng/news/2024/12/01/7487059/))
- **2015 European Youth Olympic Winter Festival:** Competed at age 16 in Tschagguns, Austria, finishing 29th in NH/10km -- his first major international event ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dmytro_Mazurchuk))

## Key Storylines
### Soldier-Athlete: From the National Guard to the Olympic Hill
Dmytro Mazurchuk has been a member of the **National Guard of Ukraine since February 2022** -- the month Russia launched its full-scale invasion ([Wikidata](https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Q28867093)). While the exact nature of his duties is not publicly detailed, the fact that he has balanced military obligations with international sporting competition is extraordinary. He is, in the most literal sense, fighting for his country on two fronts: on the battlefield and on the ski hill.

### Ukraine's Nordic Combined Flag Bearer
Mazurchuk is the most accomplished Nordic Combined athlete in Ukrainian history. His Beijing 2022 Olympic appearance, his Universiade bronze medal, his World Championship participation, and his consistent World Cup presence make him the standard-bearer for a discipline that barely exists in Ukraine. Only a handful of Ukrainians have ever scored World Cup points in Nordic Combined: Mazurchuk, Shumbarets, Prosvirin, Trachuk, and Pasichnyk ([Ukrainska Pravda](https://www.pravda.com.ua/eng/news/2024/12/01/7487059/)).

### The Kremenets Connection
Born in Kremenets, a historic city in western Ukraine's Ternopil Oblast, Mazurchuk comes from a region far from traditional winter sports centers. Kremenets is known for its castle and religious architecture, not for producing ski jumpers. His path to the Olympics from this small city speaks to the power of talent identification and determination in Ukrainian sport.

### Second Olympics, New Meaning
Mazurchuk competed at Beijing 2022 just weeks before Russia's invasion. His return to the Olympics four years later carries infinitely more weight. He represents not just Ukrainian sport, but Ukrainian survival. Ukraine's 46-athlete delegation -- the largest since 2010 despite ongoing war -- is itself a statement of defiance ([Kyiv Post](https://www.kyivpost.com/post/69643)).
