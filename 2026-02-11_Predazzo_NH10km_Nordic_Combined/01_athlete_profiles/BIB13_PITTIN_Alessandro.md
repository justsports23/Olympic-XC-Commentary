## Career Highlights

  - Torino 2006: Competed at age 16 — at his first HOME Olympics ([Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/athletes/pittin))

  - Sochi 2014: **4th** (Individual NH/10km) — narrowly missed second medal ([FIS Profile](https://www.fis-ski.com/en/nordic-combined/nordic-combined-news-multimedia/news-multimedia/news/archive/athlete-profiles/article=alessandro-pittin))

  - Beijing 2022: Competed
  - Milano Cortina 2026: **6th Olympic Games — second HOME Olympics**
- **Junior World Championships:** Won gold at Zakopane 2008 ([FIS Profile](https://www.fis-ski.com/en/nordic-combined/nordic-combined-news-multimedia/news-multimedia/news/archive/athlete-profiles/article=alessandro-pittin))
- **World Cup:** Career-best 9th at Schonach 2009; multiple top-10 finishes ([FIS Results](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sectorcode=nk&competitorid=81371&type=result))
- **Injuries:** Fractured left shoulder in Klingenthal (Feb 2012), broke wrist in Ramsau training crash (Dec 2012) — came back both times ([FIS Profile](https://www.fis-ski.com/en/nordic-combined/nordic-combined-news-multimedia/news-multimedia/news/archive/athlete-profiles/article=alessandro-pittin))

## Key Storylines

### Birthday Boy
Alessandro Pittin was born on **11 February 1990** — meaning he turns **36 years old ON RACE DAY**. What a birthday present an Olympic medal would be. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alessandro_Pittin))

### Second Home Olympics — From Teenager to Legend
Pittin was just 16 years old when he competed at the 2006 Torino Winter Olympics — Italy's last home Games. Twenty years later, he returns to Italian soil for his SECOND home Olympics. "I was only 16 years old in Turin in 2006, and there has definitely been a big change in mentality in recent years," he has said. ([Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/milano-cortina-2026/news/milano-cortina-2026-fischnaller-fontana-pittin-retornaz-second-home-olympics-italy/))

Only **four Italian athletes** are competing at both their second home Olympics: Arianna Fontana (short track), Roland Fischnaller (snowboard), Joel Retornaz (curling), and Alessandro Pittin. ([Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/milano-cortina-2026/news/milano-cortina-2026-fischnaller-fontana-pittin-retornaz-second-home-olympics-italy/))

### Italy's Only-Ever Olympic Nordic Combined Medalist
Pittin remains the **only Italian to ever win an Olympic medal in Nordic Combined** — his bronze at Vancouver 2010. He's a pioneer for the sport in Italy. ([Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/athletes/pittin))

### Comeback from Double Injury
In 2012, he suffered two major injuries within months of each other — a fractured shoulder and a broken wrist. He returned both times, showing extraordinary resilience. ([FIS Profile](https://www.fis-ski.com/en/nordic-combined/nordic-combined-news-multimedia/news-multimedia/news/archive/athlete-profiles/article=alessandro-pittin))
