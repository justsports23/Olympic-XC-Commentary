# Venue History — Val di Fiemme / Predazzo / Tesero

## Overview
Val di Fiemme in Trentino, Italy, is one of the premier Nordic skiing venues in the world. The area encompasses two key facilities:
- **Predazzo Ski Jumping Stadium** (Trampolino Giuseppe Dal Ben) — for ski jumping and the jumping component of Nordic Combined
- **Cross-Country and Biathlon Center Fabio Canal** in Tesero (Lago di Tesero) — for cross-country skiing

## Major Championships Hosted

### FIS Nordic World Ski Championships
Val di Fiemme has hosted the FIS Nordic World Ski Championships **three times**:

| Year | Events | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **1991** | XC, Ski Jumping, Nordic Combined | First major international event |
| **2003** | XC, Ski Jumping, Nordic Combined | Full Nordic World Championships |
| **2013** | XC, Ski Jumping, Nordic Combined | Most recent World Championships |

### World Cup Events
Val di Fiemme has regularly hosted FIS World Cup events in Nordic Combined, cross-country skiing, and ski jumping. It is a familiar stop on the World Cup calendar.

### Tour de Ski
Val di Fiemme is famously the host of the **Tour de Ski finale** — the dramatic Final Climb stage up the Alpe Cermis. While this is a cross-country event, many Nordic Combined athletes are familiar with the valley from Tour de Ski attendance.

## 2026 Olympics — Coming Full Circle
The 2026 Milano Cortina Olympics brings Olympic events to Val di Fiemme for the first time. Despite hosting three World Championships, the valley has never previously hosted Olympic competitions. The Olympics represent the pinnacle of international sporting events for this storied venue.

## Venue Renovation for 2026
Both the Predazzo jumping hill and the Tesero cross-country stadium have undergone significant renovations and upgrades in preparation for the 2026 Olympic Games.
