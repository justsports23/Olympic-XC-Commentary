# Venue History — Tesero / Val di Fiemme

## Major Championships at Val di Fiemme

### FIS Nordic World Ski Championships

#### 2013 FIS Nordic World Ski Championships
- **Men's Skiathlon (15km + 15km):**
  - Gold: Dario Cologna (SUI)
  - Silver: Petter Northug (NOR)
  - Bronze: Johan Olsson (SWE)
- [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FIS_Nordic_World_Ski_Championships_2013)

#### 2003 FIS Nordic World Ski Championships
- Held at the same Cross Country and Biathlon Center Fabio Canal
- Notable: Korostelev's mother Natalia Korosteleva won a relay bronze medal for Russia at this championship
- [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FIS_Nordic_World_Ski_Championships_2003)

#### 1991 FIS Nordic World Ski Championships
- First World Championships held at Val di Fiemme
- [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FIS_Nordic_World_Ski_Championships_1991)

### Tour de Ski
Val di Fiemme has been the permanent home of the Tour de Ski final stages, including the iconic Alpe Cermis climb.

#### 2025–26 Tour de Ski (January 3–4, 2026 — 5 weeks before Olympics)
- **Overall Winner:** Johannes Høsflot Klæbo (NOR) — 5th title (record) [FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com)
- **Final Climb Winner:** Mattis Stenshagen (NOR) — 33:25.5 [FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com)
- **Alpe Cermis 2nd:** Jules Lapierre (FRA) [Nordic Mag](https://www.nordicmag.info)
- Nearly all top athletes in the Olympic skiathlon field raced at this venue five weeks earlier

#### 2024–25 Tour de Ski
- **Alpe Cermis Final Climb Winner:** Jules Lapierre (FRA) [FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com)
- **Alpe Cermis 2nd:** Mika Vermeulen (AUT)

### Other Events
- Multiple FIS World Cup rounds held annually
- Continental Cup and lower-tier races
- The venue underwent €22.4 million in renovations for the 2026 Games [Nordic Mag](https://www.nordicmag.info) [Olympics](https://www.olympics.com)

## Venue Specifications
- **Full Name:** Cross Country and Biathlon Center Fabio Canal
- **Location:** Tesero (Lago di Tesero), Val di Fiemme, Trentino-Alto Adige, Italy
- **Altitude:** ~830–850m above sea level
- **Renovations (2026):** Rebuilt grandstand, underground facilities, modernized snowmaking — €22.4 million investment
