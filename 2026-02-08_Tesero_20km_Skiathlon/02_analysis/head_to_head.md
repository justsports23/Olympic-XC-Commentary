# Head-to-Head Matchups — Men's 10km + 10km Skiathlon

## Matchup 1: Klæbo vs. Amundsen (The Season Rivalry)

The defining rivalry of the 2025–26 season.

| Race | Klæbo | Amundsen | Gap |
|------|-------|----------|-----|
| Trondheim Skiathlon (Dec 6) | 1st (43:49.4) | 2nd | +0.7s |
| Tour de Ski Overall | 1st | 3rd | +1:08.2 |
| Goms 20km Classic MS | 1st (48:29.0) | 3rd | +7.0s |
| Ruka 20km Freestyle MS | [DATA NOT FOUND] | 1st (44:42.5) | — |

**Season series:** Klæbo leads decisively, except at Ruka where Amundsen won.
**Olympic context:** Amundsen is the Olympic debutant; Klæbo has 5 Olympic golds.
**The decisive question:** Can Amundsen out-sprint Klæbo in a group finish? History says no — Klæbo's finishing speed is superior. Amundsen must separate before the final 500m.

## Matchup 2: Klæbo vs. Stenshagen (The Underdog Challenge)

| Race | Klæbo | Stenshagen | Gap |
|------|-------|------------|-----|
| TdS Toblach 10km Classic | 2nd | 1st (22:11.0) | -8.9s (Stenshagen ahead) |
| Tour de Ski Overall | 1st | 2nd | +30.1s |
| TdS Val di Fiemme Final Climb | 12th | 1st (33:25.5) | Significant |

**Key insight:** Stenshagen has beaten Klæbo in pure classic and climbing format. In the skiathlon's classic half, Stenshagen could build a meaningful lead. But the TdS overall (+30.1s) shows Klæbo's skating superiority eliminates the classic advantage when the race is long enough.

**The Olympic question:** Is 10km of classic enough for Stenshagen to build an insurmountable lead before 10km of skating? Almost certainly not — but Stenshagen's climbing power on the 140m loops makes this more plausible than any other matchup.

## Matchup 4: Pellegrino vs. The Norwegians (The Home Hero Bid)

| Race | Pellegrino | Best Norwegian |
|------|------------|----------------|
| 2024–25 TdS Val di Fiemme Skiathlon | 2nd | 1st (Klæbo) |
| 2025 WCH Skiathlon | 4th (bronze by 20cm) | 1st/2nd/3rd (NOR sweep possible) |
| Trondheim Skiathlon (Dec 6) | 16th (+24.3s) | 1st (Klæbo) |
| Tour de Ski Overall | 4th (+1:27.6) | 1st (Klæbo) |

**Key insight:** Pellegrino's best skiathlon results come at Val di Fiemme (2nd in TdS) and championship events (4th at Worlds). The Olympics at Val di Fiemme is the perfect convergence. His Trondheim 16th shows he can't match Norwegians on a neutral day — but this isn't a neutral day. Home crowd, home snow, final Games.
