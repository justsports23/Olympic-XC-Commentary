# Podium Contenders — Women's Sprint Classic

## The Top 5 Gold Favorites (Ranked)

### 1. Linn SVAHN (SWE) — Bib 8
**Why she wins:** The most talented pure sprinter in the field, peaking at the perfect moment. Won the most recent classic sprint (Goms, Jan 24) by a dominant margin — fastest qualifier by 1.84 seconds. Her first-ever Olympics adds emotional fuel. When healthy and firing, she is the woman to beat.

**The case against:** Concussion recovery, illness-related DNS at Oberhof in January. Has she fully recovered? The Tesero course's two sustained climbs (49m total) are more demanding than typical sprint courses — endurance sprinters may have an edge over pure speed merchants. And this is her first-ever Olympic final — championship pressure is different.

**Key stat:** 40% win rate in World Cup sprint starts. 63% podium rate. When she races, she almost always wins or podiums.

---

### 2. Jonna SUNDLING (SWE) — Bib 24
**Why she wins:** The greatest big-race sprinter of her generation. Three consecutive World Championship sprint titles. Defending Olympic champion. Her selective season approach — skipping the TdS to peak for the Olympics — has produced 3 straight World sprint titles and an Olympic gold. She has NEVER failed to medal at a championship she's targeted since 2021.

**The case against:** Her Oberhof win and Davos win were both in freestyle — this is a classic sprint. Her 2nd at Ruka (classic) is her only classic sprint result this season. At 31, the cumulative fatigue of 5 rounds may test her. She hasn't raced the Olympic course.

**Key stat:** 3 consecutive World sprint titles + 1 Olympic gold in the last 5 years. The ultimate championship closer.

---

### 3. Jasmi JOENSUU (FIN) — Bib 6
**Why she wins:** Won on this exact course five weeks ago. FIS sprint points rank 2 globally. Her classic sprint profile — combining sprint speed with distance endurance — perfectly matches the demanding Tesero course. She broke away on the final climb at the TdS, proving the hill is her weapon.

**The case against:** The Olympic final is a different beast from a TdS stage. Her Goms 9th (most recent sprint result) shows she can have off-days in knockout rounds. Several top sprinters (Sundling, Svahn, Skistad) were absent from the TdS — the TdS field was weaker than the Olympic field.

**Key stat:** Won qualifying AND the final on the Olympic course (Jan 3, 2026). 3:45.75. She knows every meter.

---

### 4. Nadine FÄHNDRICH (SUI) — Bib 4
**Why she wins:** 2nd on the Olympic course at the TdS (0.44s behind Joensuu). #2 in Sprint WC standings. Self-described classic specialist — the technique match is perfect. Won the 2025 TdS classic sprint at this venue. Had heart surgery in 2024 and came back racing 2 weeks later — mental toughness is not in question.

**The case against:** Her Oberhof 16th (freestyle) shows she's not as strong in freestyle — but this race is classic, so it may not matter. She's been 2nd and 3rd rather than winning classic sprints this season.

**Key stat:** Won the 2025 TdS classic sprint AND finished 2nd in the 2026 TdS classic sprint — both at Val di Fiemme. Two-year classic sprint dominance at this venue.

---

### 5. Maja DAHLQVIST (SWE) — Bib 18
**Why she wins:** Sprint World Cup leader with 525 points. Made the classic sprint final in ALL FOUR classic sprints this season. Olympic sprint silver medalist (Beijing 2022). Incredibly consistent — she's always in the mix and can never be dismissed.

**The case against:** She finishes 3rd-4th in classic sprints rather than winning them (3rd, 4th, 4th, 4th). The Tesero climb profile may further disadvantage her finishing-speed style. She hasn't won a classic sprint this season or recently.

**Key stat:** 4/4 classic sprint finals this season with results of 3rd, 4th, 4th, 4th. The most consistent but not the most dangerous.

---

## Additional Strong Contenders (6-8)

### 6. Johanna HAGSTRÖM (SWE) — Bib 28
3rd on the Olympic course (TdS). Won Trondheim classic sprint. Sweden's strongest classic sprint performer this season by results. Front-runner who needs clean air.

### 7. Kristine Stavaas SKISTAD (NOR) — Bib 14
13 World Cup wins. Ruka classic sprint winner. 2025 WC sprint silver. But hasn't raced in 43 days due to illness and has NOT raced on the Olympic course. The biggest unknown.

### 8. Laura GIMMLER (GER) — Bib 12
Career-best 2nd at Goms classic sprint (Jan 24). In the form of her life at 32. Lost to Svahn by just **0.12 seconds in the A Final** — [FIS Results](https://medias4.fis-ski.com/pdf/2026/CC/2241/2026CC2241RL.pdf).
