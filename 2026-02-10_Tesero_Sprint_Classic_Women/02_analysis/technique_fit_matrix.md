# Technique Fit Matrix — Women's Sprint Classic

## Race Demands Assessment

The Tesero Olympic Sprint Classic demands:
1. **Strong diagonal stride** on the sustained 23m climb (critical)
2. **Endurance** for 5 potential rounds over 1,547m with 49m vertical per run
3. **Classic sprint speed** — not just technique, but pace
4. **Wax reliability** — kick wax in zero-zone conditions
5. **Tactical awareness** — positioning, pacing across two climbs
6. **Championship mentality** — performing under Olympic pressure

## Key Takeaways

### Best Classic Technique Fit
1. **Joensuu** — classic sprint specialist; won on this course
2. **Fähndrich** — self-described classic specialist; 2 podiums on this course
3. **Hagström** — both WC wins in classic sprint

### Best Climb Fit
1. **Joensuu** — broke away on the Tesero climb at TdS
2. **Matintalo** — 800m runner's VO2 max; born climber
3. **Diggins** — 3x Tour de Ski champion (Alpe Cermis climber)

### Best Endurance Fit (5 Rounds)
1. **Diggins** — WC overall leader; distance skier by trade
2. **Matintalo** — first WC win was in 20km classic
3. **Sundling/Dahlqvist** — both raced full TdS or strategic rest

### Best Pure Sprint Speed
1. **Svahn** — fastest qualifier at Goms by 1.84 seconds
2. **Skistad** — explosive acceleration, 13 WC wins
3. **Sundling/Dahlqvist** — proven sprint finishers

### Best Wax Team Advantage
1. **Sweden (Svahn/Sundling/Dahlqvist/Hagström)** — "super skis" at skiathlon
2. **Finland (Joensuu/Matintalo/Kahara/Saari)** — "speedy skis" at TdS

### Best Championship Pedigree
1. **Sundling** — 3 WC titles + Olympic gold; never fails at championships
2. **Diggins** — Olympic gold + bronze; 3x Tour champion
3. **Skistad** — WC sprint silver; 2019 Junior World Champion

## The Verdict
**Joensuu and Sundling** have the highest overall fit scores, but for different reasons: Joensuu's classic technique and climb strength perfectly match the course; Sundling's sprint speed and championship mentality overcome any technique gaps. **Svahn** is the most dangerous if healthy — her raw speed is the highest in the field, and her Goms win proves her classic is strong enough.

The athletes most **disadvantaged** by this course: Rydzek (classic weakness), Dahlqvist (climb neutralizes finishing speed), and pure freestyle sprinters in general.
