# Venue History — Val di Fiemme / Tesero

## Overview
Val di Fiemme (Tesero/Cavalese) is one of the most storied cross-country skiing venues in the world. The **Centro Fondo Fabio Canal** in Tesero has hosted multiple World Championships, Tour de Ski stages, and now the 2026 Olympic Games.

### Tour de Ski
Val di Fiemme has been the traditional Tour de Ski finale venue, featuring:
- Sprint races at the Tesero stadium
- The famous **Alpe Cermis final climb** (not part of the sprint course)

### World Cup Events
Val di Fiemme has regularly hosted World Cup distance and sprint events, particularly as part of the Tour de Ski.

## Historical Significance — Franco Nones
**Franco Nones**, born in Val di Fiemme itself, won Italy's **first-ever Olympic cross-country skiing gold medal** at the 1968 Grenoble Olympics (**30km**) — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franco_Nones). His victory put Val di Fiemme on the map as the spiritual home of Italian cross-country skiing. The venue bears his legacy.

## 2013 World Championships — Diggins' First Gold
At the 2013 Nordic World Ski Championships in Val di Fiemme, **Jessie Diggins** and **Kikkan Randall** won the team sprint — America's first-ever World Championship gold in cross-country skiing. Diggins returns to this venue 13 years later for her final Olympics.
