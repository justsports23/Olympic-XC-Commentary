## Career Summary
Hugo Hinckfuss is a two-time Olympian and one of Australia's most exciting cross-country ski prospects. Born in Brisbane and raised in Sydney, he became the [youngest Australian ever to compete in cross-country skiing at an Olympics](https://www.olympics.com.au/olympians/hugo-hinckfuss/) when he raced at Beijing 2022 at age 18 ([Australian Olympic Committee](https://www.olympics.com.au/olympians/hugo-hinckfuss/)). He finished 61st in the sprint freestyle and 81st in the 15 km classic at Beijing ([Australian Olympic Committee](https://www.olympics.com.au/olympians/hugo-hinckfuss/)). At Milano Cortina 2026, he recorded a best of 42nd in the Men's Sprint Classic ([FIS Athlete Profile](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sectorcode=cc&competitorid=229979)). His Junior World Championship career highlights include qualifying 10th in the sprint classic at Whistler 2023 (first Australian junior in top 10 sprint qualifying) and a best of 26th at Lygna 2022 — both reflecting Australia's most successful Junior Worlds campaign to date ([Snow Australia](https://www.snow.org.au/news/dual-olympians-make-their-start)).

## Personal Background
Hinckfuss was born in Brisbane and grew up in Sydney ([Australian Olympic Committee](https://www.olympics.com.au/olympians/hugo-hinckfuss/)). He [discovered cross-country skiing](https://cubuffs.com/sports/skiing/roster/hugo-hinckfuss/16731) when teachers encouraged his Year 5 class to form an interschools ski team — an unusually organic introduction to the sport for a future Olympian ([University of Colorado Athletics](https://cubuffs.com/sports/skiing/roster/hugo-hinckfuss/16731)). He is a fluent Polish speaker [UNVERIFIED]. He enrolled at the [University of Colorado](https://cubuffs.com/sports/skiing/roster/hugo-hinckfuss/16731) in Boulder, initially studying civil engineering and later shifting focus to Applied Mathematics, where he has earned academic honours ([University of Colorado Athletics](https://cubuffs.com/sports/skiing/roster/hugo-hinckfuss/16731)). He took a semester off from university to focus on Olympic qualification for Milano Cortina 2026 ([CU Buffs](https://cubuffs.com/news/2026/1/23/skiing-two-more-buffs-headed-to-milan-cortina-as-hinckfuss-gaskill-added-to-olympic-roster)). He aims to apply mathematics and physiology to high-performance sport, using data to find "marginal gains" ([University of Colorado Athletics](https://cubuffs.com/sports/skiing/roster/hugo-hinckfuss/16731)).
