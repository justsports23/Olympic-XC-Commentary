The men's team sprint was introduced to the World Championships programme in 2005 at Oberstdorf.

## Summary: Norway's Team Sprint Dominance

| Year | Event | Gold |
|------|-------|------|
| 2006 | Olympics Turin (Free) | Sweden (Fredriksson/Lind) |
| 2010 | Olympics Vancouver (Free) | Norway (Pettersen/Northug) |
| 2014 | Olympics Sochi (Classic) | Finland (Niskanen/Jauhojärvi) |
| 2017 | Worlds Lahti (Free) | Norway |
| 2018 | Olympics PyeongChang (Free) | Norway (Sundby/Klæbo) |
| 2019 | Worlds Seefeld (Classic) | Norway (Iversen/Klæbo) |
| 2021 | Worlds Oberstdorf (Free) | Norway (Klæbo/partner) |
| 2022 | Olympics Beijing (Classic) | Norway (Valnes/Klæbo) |
| 2023 | Worlds Planica (Free) | Norway (Golberg/Klæbo) |
| 2025 | Worlds Trondheim (Classic) | Norway (Valnes/Klæbo) |
| 2026 | Olympics Tesero (Free) | TBD |

Norway has won 7 of the last 9 major team sprint titles (Olympic + World Championships) from 2010 to 2025. Finland's 2014 gold (Sochi) and Sweden's 2006 gold (Turin) are the only exceptions. Klæbo has personally won gold in the last five consecutive major team sprint events he has entered.
