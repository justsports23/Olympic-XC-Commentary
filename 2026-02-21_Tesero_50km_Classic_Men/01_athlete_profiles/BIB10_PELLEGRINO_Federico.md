## Personal Background

Federico Pellegrino is an Italian sprint legend from Aosta, in the shadow of Mont Blanc in the Aosta Valley. His passion for cross-country skiing was inherited from his father, who became so passionate about the sport after returning from the 1994 Lillehammer Olympics that he passed on his love for the discipline to his son — [Bella Italia Goods](https://bellaitaliagoods.com/federico-pellegrino-italian-cross-country-skiing-champion/).

Pellegrino and his wife, retired Olympic cross-country skier Greta Laurent, welcomed their second child in April 2025 — [olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/milano-cortina-2026/news/federico-pellegrino-exclusive-milano-cortina-2026-cross-country-skiing).

This is Pellegrino's final season as a professional skier. He has set his retirement date for February 22, 2026 — the day after the 50km classic, making this race his career finale. He has described the Milano Cortina Olympics as *"the ultimate goal of my career"* — [olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/news/exclusive-federico-pellegrino-cross-country-ski-mico-2026-ultimate-goal).

On his ambitions for the home Games, he told FIS: *"They are ready. My dream was not just to see me winning medals, but to see Italy on the podium in the team event"* — [FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/cross-country/news/2025-26/pellegrino-dreams-of-italian-olympic-medals-with-or-without-him-they-are-ready).

The relay bronze — Italy's first Olympic relay medal since the legendary 2006 Turin gold — moved Pellegrino to tears at Tesero — [Nordic Mag](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/2026-olympics-cross-country-skiing-federico-pellegrinos-tears-0215/).

## Technique & Format Profile
- **Classic strength:** Good for a sprint specialist. His Olympic sprint silver at PyeongChang was in classic technique. However, classic distance racing at 50km is a very different challenge from classic sprinting.
- **Freestyle strength:** Elite in sprint format. Beijing 2022 sprint silver was in free technique. His finishing kick in freestyle sprints is one of the fastest in history.
- **Sprint ability:** All-time great. One of the most decorated sprint specialists in World Cup history with 18+ individual wins, 2 Crystal Globes, 1 World Championship gold, 2 Olympic silver medals.
- **Distance preference:** Sprint specialist by nature. The 50km classic is vastly outside his comfort zone — he has never previously raced a 50km at the Olympics.
- **Best format:** Sprint (individual and team) — overwhelming strength.

## Key Storylines

### The Farewell Race
The 50km classic mass start is Federico Pellegrino's final race as a professional skier. His retirement date of February 22, 2026, falls the day after this event. A career that began with his World Cup debut in 2010 — spanning four Olympic Games, two Olympic silver medals, a World Championship gold, and over 60 World Cup podiums — will end on the snow at Tesero, in his home country, in front of the Italian tifosi — [olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/milano-cortina-2026/news/federico-pellegrino-exclusive-milano-cortina-2026-cross-country-skiing).

### From Sprinter to Marathon Man
Pellegrino's decision to race the 50km classic as his career finale is remarkable. He is the most decorated sprint specialist to attempt the Olympic 50km in recent memory. His 2nd-place finish in a 20km Skiathlon during the Tour de Ski — a format he had never previously cracked the top 30 in — showed shocking distance form. But 50km is 50km, and the classic technique demands a different kind of strength than his sprint mastery provides — [FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/cross-country/news/2025-26/pellegrino-dreams-of-italian-olympic-medals-with-or-without-him-they-are-ready).

### The Tears at Tesero
The relay bronze — Italy's first Olympic relay medal since the legendary 2006 Turin gold at Pragelato — moved Pellegrino to visible tears at the Tesero stadium. For a man who has won World Championship gold and two Olympic silver medals, the team success at home carried a different emotional weight — [Nordic Mag](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/2026-olympics-cross-country-skiing-federico-pellegrinos-tears-0215/).

### Home Crowd Hero
Racing in the Italian Dolomites, across the mountains from his hometown of Aosta, Pellegrino will have massive crowd support. The Tesero stadium has been electric for Italian athletes throughout these Games. As Italy's most recognizable cross-country skier, the atmosphere for his final race will be extraordinary.

### The Father's Legacy
Pellegrino's father fell in love with cross-country skiing after the 1994 Lillehammer Olympics and passed that passion to his son. For Pellegrino to end his career at a home Olympics — 32 years after the Games that inspired his father — is a powerful circular narrative — [Bella Italia Goods](https://bellaitaliagoods.com/federico-pellegrino-italian-cross-country-skiing-champion/).
