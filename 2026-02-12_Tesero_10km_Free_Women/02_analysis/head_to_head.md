# Head-to-Head Matchups: Women's 10km Interval Start Free

**Event:** Olympic Winter Games Milano Cortina 2026
**Venue:** Tesero Cross-Country Skiing Stadium, Val di Fiemme, Italy
**Date:** THU 12 FEB 2026 | 13:00 CET
**Format:** 10km Interval Start Freestyle | 3 laps | 390m total climb | ~960m altitude

---

## 1. Jessie Diggins (USA, BIB 44) vs. Frida Karlsson (SWE, BIB 42)

**The Marquee Matchup: Defending World Cup Champion vs. Reigning Olympic Skiathlon Gold Medalist**

This is the defining rivalry of women's cross-country skiing in the 2025-26 season. Diggins leads the Overall World Cup standings (1,563 pts); Karlsson just won the most dominant Olympic performance of the Games so far with a 51-second skiathlon victory.

### Season Head-to-Head

| Date | Event | Location | Format | Diggins | Karlsson | Winner |
|------|-------|----------|--------|---------|----------|--------|
| Nov 28, 2025 | 10km Classic IS | Ruka (FIN) | Interval Start | [DATA NOT FOUND] | **1st** | Karlsson |
| Nov 30, 2025 | 20km Free MS | Ruka (FIN) | Mass Start | **2nd** | 5th (+4.9s) | Diggins |
| Dec 7, 2025 | 10km Free IS | Trondheim (NOR) | Interval Start | 3rd | 8th (+29.9s) | Diggins ahead |
| Jan 3, 2026 | TdS Sprint Classic | Val di Fiemme (ITA) | Sprint | 7th | 17th | Diggins ahead |
| Feb 7, 2026 | **Olympic Skiathlon** | **Tesero (ITA)** | **Mass Start** | **8th** (+2:21.1) | **1st (GOLD)** | **Karlsson** |

**Season series: Split -- each has won key battles, but Karlsson holds the momentum with skiathlon gold.**

### Historical Context

- Diggins and Karlsson have been the two dominant women in distance skiing since 2021, trading the top spot across World Cup seasons.
- Diggins has won 3 consecutive Overall Crystal Globes (2021, 2024, 2026 in progress); Karlsson has won the Tour de Ski (2023) and the 50km World Championship gold (Trondheim 2025).
- At the Beijing 2022 Olympics, Diggins won silver in the 30km and bronze in the sprint; Karlsson DNS'd the 30km due to illness after finishing 5th in the skiathlon.
- Both are elite freestyle distance skiers. Karlsson has two World Championship silvers in the 10km freestyle (2021 Oberstdorf, 2023 Planica). Diggins is the reigning World Cup distance leader.
- **Critical difference at this venue:** Karlsson dominated the Tesero course 5 days ago, pulling away in the freestyle leg of the skiathlon. Diggins was caught behind a crash and finished 8th -- she will be desperate for redemption in a race with no pack dynamics.

### Key Factors for the 10km Free

- **Interval start favors Diggins:** No crashes, no pack tactics -- just pure fitness and pacing. Diggins excels in time-trial formats. Her BIB 44 gives her full split-time information on the field.
- **Karlsson's confidence is sky-high:** She told HBO Max after the skiathlon: "I felt like a machine." The question is whether her recurring foot injury allows back-to-back peak performances in 5 days.
- **Climbing course suits both:** Diggins is a 3x Tour de Ski champion (Alpe Cermis climber). Karlsson won the Tour de Ski in 2023 and dominated the skiathlon's climbing sections.
- **Foot injury factor:** Karlsson withdrew from the Tour de Ski final stage (Jan 4) with a foot problem. The skiathlon gold suggests she is healthy, but a second hard effort on this demanding course will test that foot.

**Verdict:** This is a genuine toss-up. Diggins has the World Cup pedigree and interval start expertise; Karlsson has the Olympic momentum and a fresh demonstration of dominance on this exact course. ([FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/cross-country/news/2025-26/karlsson-storms-to-first-cross-country-gold-at-milano-cortina), [NBC Olympics](https://www.nbcolympics.com/news/jessie-diggins-announces-retirement-2026-olympics-will-be-last-cross-country-skier))

## 2. Ebba Andersson (SWE, BIB 40) vs. Frida Karlsson (SWE, BIB 42)

**Swedish Teammates, Swedish Rivals**

Andersson and Karlsson are the twin pillars of Swedish women's cross-country skiing -- both train with Solleftea Skidor IF, both represent Sweden's A-team, and both are among the most decorated skiers in World Championship history. But the internal rivalry is fierce.

### Season Head-to-Head

| Date | Event | Location | Format | Andersson | Karlsson | Winner |
|------|-------|----------|--------|-----------|----------|--------|
| Nov 28, 2025 | 10km Classic IS | Ruka (FIN) | Interval Start | 4th | **1st** | Karlsson |
| Nov 30, 2025 | 20km Free MS | Ruka (FIN) | Mass Start | 5th (+4.0) | 5th (+4.9) | Andersson (marginally) |
| Dec 7, 2025 | 10km Free IS | Trondheim (NOR) | Interval Start | **1st** (26:05.3) | 8th (+29.9) | **Andersson** |
| Feb 7, 2026 | **Olympic Skiathlon** | **Tesero (ITA)** | **Mass Start** | **2nd (SILVER)** | **1st (GOLD)** | **Karlsson by 51s** |

**Season series: Karlsson 2, Andersson 2 -- but Karlsson won the biggest race of the year by 51 seconds.**

### Historical Rivalry

- At the 2025 Trondheim World Championships, Andersson won 3 individual golds (Skiathlon, 10km Classic, Relay), while Karlsson won the 50km gold and Relay gold. In the 50km, Andersson fell when she stumbled into Karlsson's skis while in medal contention, finishing 4th. The incident was a painful moment in the rivalry. ([Sweden Herald](https://swedenherald.com/article/barely-able-to-hold-back-tears))
- Andersson is the more decorated World Championship athlete (11 medals: 6G, 1S, 4B) compared to Karlsson (13 medals: 3G, 5S, 5B), but Karlsson now has the Olympic gold.
- Both come from athletics backgrounds: Andersson competed at the 2015 European Junior Championships in the 3000m; Karlsson was a 1500m/3000m runner before switching to skiing at 17.
- **Trondheim 10km Free IS (Dec 7, 2025):** Andersson beat Karlsson by nearly 30 seconds in the exact same race format as the Olympic 10km. This is the most directly relevant head-to-head result.

### Key Factors for the 10km Free

- **Andersson's format specialty:** 7 of her 9 World Cup wins have come at 10km distance. Her most recent win (Trondheim, Dec 7, 2025) was a 10km Interval Start Free -- this exact format. She is the reigning 10km specialist.
- **Karlsson's fitness question:** After the skiathlon's 20km effort, how much does Karlsson have left? Andersson also raced the skiathlon (silver, +51.0) but was not pushing at Karlsson's extreme level.
- **Unconventional preparation:** Andersson trained at her parents' home in Solleftea after Tour de Ski, skipping all World Cup racing from January 4 onwards. This same strategy preceded her 3-gold haul at the 2025 Trondheim Worlds. ([FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/cross-country/news/2025-26/andersson-leads-swedish-party-in-skiathlon-at-milano-cortina))
- **The broken medal:** Andersson's silver medal from the skiathlon physically broke apart after the ceremony. She'll want a gold that stays intact. ([Nordic Mag](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/2026-olympics-cross-country-skiing-there-are-still-pieces-of-it-lying-around-in-the-snow-the-misadventure-of-ebba-andersson-second-in-the-skiathlon-with-her-medal-0207/))

**Verdict:** Andersson is the more proven 10km interval start performer. Karlsson's dominance in the skiathlon was undeniable, but the interval start removes mass-start dynamics where Karlsson thrives. Andersson has the format edge; Karlsson has the confidence edge. ([NBC Olympics](https://www.nbcolympics.com/news/ebba-andersson-earns-first-world-cup-victory-season-10km-free-jessie-diggins-clinches-3rd), [ProXCskiing](https://www.proxcskiing.com/traditional-skiing/sweden-storms-to-skiathlon-gold-and-silver-at-milano-cortina-2026/))

## 3. Finnish Veterans: Kerttu Niskanen (FIN, BIB 60) vs. Krista Parmakoski (FIN, BIB 28)

**Two Legends in Their Final Olympic Games**

Niskanen (37) and Parmakoski (35) have been the twin engines of Finnish women's cross-country skiing for over a decade. Both are competing in their likely final Olympics. Between them, they hold 17 Olympic and World Championship medals.

### Season Head-to-Head

| Date | Event | Location | Format | Niskanen | Parmakoski | Winner |
|------|-------|----------|--------|----------|------------|--------|
| Nov 28, 2025 | 10km Classic IS | Ruka (FIN) | Interval Start | 19th | 10th | Parmakoski |
| Dec 2025 | Tour de Ski 10km C | Toblach (ITA) | Interval Start | **4th** | 38th | **Niskanen** |
| Jan 25, 2026 | 20km Classic MS | Goms (SUI) | Mass Start | **4th** (+7.4) | [Not entered] | Niskanen |
| Feb 7, 2026 | **Olympic Skiathlon** | **Tesero (ITA)** | **Mass Start** | **5th** | **~20th** (+4:01.5) | **Niskanen** |

**Season series: Niskanen has been clearly the stronger of the two this season, with a Tour de Ski 4th, Goms 4th, and Olympic skiathlon 5th.**

### Career Parallel

- **Niskanen:** 4 Olympic medals (3 Silver, 1 Bronze). Her Beijing 2022 silver in the 10km Classic lost gold to Johaug by just 0.4 seconds. 8 World Cup victories (all in classic). Distance Cup champion 2022-23.
- **Parmakoski:** 5 Olympic medals (2 Silver, 3 Bronze). Won bronze in the 10km Freestyle at PyeongChang 2018. 6 World Cup victories. 7 World Championship medals. Finland's flag bearer at the Milano Cortina 2026 opening ceremony.
- Both anchored the Finnish 4x5km relay to silver at the 2014 Sochi Olympics and have been relay partners for over a decade.
- **Key distinction:** Niskanen is historically a classic specialist (all 8 WC wins in classic) while Parmakoski is more versatile (Olympic bronze in 10km freestyle, WC wins in both techniques). For a freestyle race, Parmakoski's technique background may be advantageous despite her weaker form.

### Key Factors for the 10km Free

- **Niskanen's freestyle improvement:** Historically her weaker technique, but she placed 5th in the Olympic skiathlon (which included a freestyle half) and has been improving in freestyle dramatically in recent seasons, earning her first freestyle podiums in 2025 at age 36.
- **Parmakoski's championship pedigree:** Her 12 major championship medals demonstrate an ability to peak when it matters most. Her 20th-place skiathlon suggests her form is limited, but she has a history of surprise performances at championships.
- **Both know Val di Fiemme intimately:** Parmakoski won a World Championship team sprint bronze at this venue in 2013 and has raced multiple Tour de Ski editions here. Niskanen raced the Tour de Ski here in both 2025 and 2026, plus the Olympic skiathlon.
- **Farewell tour emotion:** Both athletes are in their final competitive season. The emotional weight of competing in their last Olympic individual race could elevate or inhibit performance.

**Verdict:** Niskanen is the stronger performer this season and should finish ahead. Top 10 is realistic for Niskanen; Parmakoski may struggle to crack the top 15 but has the championship DNA to surprise. ([FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/cross-country/news/2025-26/ruka-women-s-10km), [Nordic Mag](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/2026-olympics-cross-country-skiing-krista-parmakoski-named-finlands-flag-bearer-for-her-last-olympic-games-0202/))

**Four Norwegians, Four Different Stories**

Norway has a deep squad for this event, with each athlete bringing distinct strengths and recent Olympic history from the skiathlon five days earlier.

In the Olympic skiathlon, Weng and Slind's fortunes diverged dramatically in the freestyle half. Slind was among the three leaders at the ski change alongside Karlsson and Andersson, but she could not sustain the pace in freestyle. Weng, starting 8th at the transition, flew past Slind in the skating section to claim bronze.

**Key dynamic:** Weng is the stronger freestyle skier; Slind is the stronger classic skier. In a freestyle race, Weng holds the advantage. Slind's marathon background gives her extraordinary pacing ability but her Olympic skiathlon showed that pure freestyle speed against the best is her limitation. #### Simpson-Larsen: The Breakthrough Star

Simpson-Larsen has the most impressive freestyle credentials among the Norwegians. She won the 10km Interval Start Free at Davos (Dec 14, 2025) -- the exact same format and technique as this Olympic race -- beating Ilar by 2.1 seconds. She also won the Tour de Ski Final Climb at Val di Fiemme, beating Diggins by 8.8 seconds on this very course.

|------|-------|--------|--------|-------|
| Dec 14, 2025 | Davos 10km Free IS | Interval Start | **1st** | Beat Ilar by 2.1s |
| Jan 4, 2026 | TdS Final Climb | Mass Start | **1st** | Beat Diggins by 8.8s at Val di Fiemme |
| Feb 7, 2026 | Olympic Skiathlon | Mass Start | 13th | Below expectations |

Her 13th-place skiathlon was disappointing, but the skiathlon's classic half is not her strength. In a pure freestyle race, Simpson-Larsen is arguably Norway's strongest card. ([FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/cross-country/news/2025-26/simpson-larsen-in-shock-after-first-world-cup-victory))

#### Fosnaes: The Rising Star

At 25, Fosnaes is the youngest of the Norwegian quartet and in only her second season on the elite team. Her 11th-place Olympic skiathlon was a strong debut. She won a national race at Steinkjer in January and took 2nd at the World Cup opener in Beitostoelen (10km Classic). The 10km free is less proven territory for her than classic, but her 12th-place finish in the Trondheim 10km Free IS (Dec 7) shows she can be competitive. ([ProXCskiing](https://www.proxcskiing.com/traditional-skiing/fosnaes-crushed-the-competition-almost-beat-all-the-men-at-pinaslopet/))

### Norwegian Medal Probabilities (for 10km Free)

1. **Simpson-Larsen** -- Highest freestyle ceiling. Won a 10km Free IS this season. Won on this course. The strongest Norwegian medal chance.
2. **Weng** -- Olympic bronze momentum. Outstanding freestyle ability. Holds the Val di Fiemme Tour de Ski final climb record (18:02.1). Knows this course as well as anyone.
3. **Slind** -- Has won freestyle races this season (Toblach 20km F, Engadin 20km F) but struggled in the skiathlon's skating leg. The 10km distance may be too short for her marathon-specialist strengths.
4. **Fosnaes** -- Top-15 is a realistic target. An outside medal shot would require a career-best performance.

**Verdict:** Simpson-Larsen is Norway's most dangerous entry for the podium. Weng is the safest bet for a top-8 result. Slind and Fosnaes will fight for positions in the 8-15 range. ([FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/cross-country/news/2025-26/-he-was-with-me-today-simpson-larsen-wins-final-climb-for-late-biathlete-bakken), [FasterSkier](https://fasterskier.com/2026/01/the-cost-of-being-the-best-norway-finally-locks-in-its-olympic-squad/))

## 5. Jessie Diggins (USA, BIB 44) vs. Dariya Nepryaeva (AIN, BIB 29)

**The Season-Long Rivalry Meets Olympic Politics**

This matchup carries both sporting and political dimensions. Diggins is the World Cup overall leader pursuing her farewell Olympic gold. Nepryaeva is a 23-year-old competing under the "Individual Neutral Athlete" designation after CAS overturned FIS's ban on Russian athletes in December 2025.

### Season Head-to-Head

Direct head-to-head results are limited because Nepryaeva only returned to international competition at Davos in December 2025 after nearly three years of competition ban.

| Date | Event | Location | Format | Diggins | Nepryaeva | Winner |
|------|-------|----------|--------|---------|-----------|--------|
| Jan 2026 | Goms 20km Classic MS | Goms (SUI) | Mass Start | [Not entered] | 8th | Nepryaeva |
| Feb 7, 2026 | **Olympic Skiathlon** | **Tesero (ITA)** | **Mass Start** | **8th** (+2:21.1) | **17th** | **Diggins** |

**Season series: Limited data. Diggins is clearly the stronger athlete at this point in their respective careers.**

### Context and Significance

- **Natalya Nepryaeva (Dariya's older sister)** won 3 medals at the Beijing 2022 Olympics -- an event that Russian athletes controversially competed in before the full FIS ban took effect. One of Natalya's Beijing medals was a bronze in the 10km Classic, where she was beaten for the podium's top spots by Johaug and Niskanen and held off Parmakoski by just 0.1 seconds.
- **Dariya lost nearly three years** of development (ages 20-23) to the competition ban. Her FIS Distance Points of 24.87 (Rank 28) show she was a legitimate distance talent before the ban.
- **Diggins' advocacy work** -- as a prominent athlete voice, Diggins has spoken about the integrity of clean competition and fair play, themes that intersect with the broader debate about neutral athlete participation.
- **The competitive gap:** Diggins is a tier above Nepryaeva based on current form. The rivalry is more about the broader narrative of these Olympics than a direct medal fight. However, Nepryaeva at 23 has significant upside if she can regain the development time lost to the ban.

### Key Factors for the 10km Free

- **Nepryaeva's best format:** The 10km freestyle is a better fit than the skiathlon (which required a classic half she was less prepared for). Her FIS Distance Points suggest she belongs in the competitive tier of this event.
- **Diggins' motivation:** An individual Olympic gold is the one missing piece in the most legendary career in American XC skiing. She does not need motivation -- but racing against neutral athletes under the current political backdrop adds another layer to the narrative.
- **Realistic outcome:** Nepryaeva targets top 15-20; Diggins targets the podium. Their paths cross in the broader context of these Games rather than in a direct medal fight.

**Verdict:** This is not a close sporting matchup -- Diggins is significantly stronger. But it is one of the defining storylines of these Olympics, and every split-time comparison between them will be loaded with meaning beyond the race itself. ([FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/cross-country/results.html))
