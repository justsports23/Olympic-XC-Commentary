# Podium Contenders -- Women's 10km Interval Start Free
**Event:** Milano Cortina 2026 Olympic Winter Games
**Date:** THU 12 FEB 2026 | **Venue:** Tesero Cross-Country Skiing Stadium, Val di Fiemme, Italy

**Course:** 3 laps (Red 3.75km + Blue 3.75km + Blue 2.5km) | **Total Climb:** 390m | **Technique:** Freestyle (Skating)

---

## 1. Jessie Diggins (USA) -- BIB 44

### Why She's a Contender

Jessie Diggins is the single most compelling favorite for this race. The 10km interval start freestyle is, quite simply, her event. She is the reigning World Cup Overall leader (1,563 pts, leading by 223 points), the reigning Distance World Cup leader, a three-time Tour de Ski champion (including January 2026), and the 2023 World Champion in the 10km freestyle at Planica. No active athlete has a resume that matches hers in this specific discipline and format.

**Current form:** Diggins has been the dominant force in women's cross-country skiing all season. She won her third Tour de Ski title in January 2026, clinching it at this very venue in Val di Fiemme. Her consistency across all formats -- distance, skiathlon, freestyle, classic -- has been extraordinary. Her 33 career World Cup victories and 88 podiums speak to a depth of experience that no one in this field can match. ([NBC Olympics](https://www.nbcolympics.com/news/how-watch-jessie-diggins-2026-winter-olympics))

**Technique fit:** Freestyle distance is Diggins' primary technique. The majority of her career success has come in skating, including her Beijing 2022 30km freestyle silver and her Planica 2023 10km freestyle world title. The interval start format eliminates crash risk and pack dynamics -- it is a pure fitness-and-pacing test, which is exactly where Diggins thrives. ([Team USA](https://www.teamusa.com/news/2026/february/01/olympic-winter-games-milano-cortina-2026-preview-cross-country-skiing))

**Course suitability:** The Tesero course's 390m of total climbing across just under 10km (39.2m/km) rewards climbers and all-around athletes. Diggins is a three-time Tour de Ski champion who has conquered the Alpe Cermis final climb multiple times. Her climbing ability is elite. The mix of one steep ascent (46m on the Red loop) and distributed undulations (Blue loops) matches her versatile engine perfectly. ([FasterSkier](https://fasterskier.com/2026/02/2026-olympic-viewers-guide/))

**Venue history:** Diggins won the 2013 World Championship team sprint gold at this venue with Kikkan Randall. She raced here during the Tour de Ski in January 2026 (7th in the Val di Fiemme sprint classic stage). She won the Tour de Ski overall at this venue. She knows these trails intimately. ([JessieDiggins.com](https://jessiediggins.com/about-2/))

**Tactical advantage:** BIB 44 means Diggins starts late in the field. She will have complete split-time data from virtually every contender before she pushes off at 13:22. For the World Cup leader and a master of pacing, this is a significant strategic edge.

**Motivation:** This is Diggins' fourth and final Olympic Games. She has announced her retirement at the end of the 2025-26 season. An individual Olympic gold is the one achievement missing from the most decorated career in American cross-country skiing history. She holds gold (team sprint 2018), silver (30km 2022), and bronze (sprint 2022) -- but never an individual Olympic title. The desire to complete that collection in her farewell will fuel extraordinary effort. ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/02/02/nx-s1-5615323/in-her-last-olympic-season-us-cross-country-ski-phenom-jessie-diggins-looks-stronger-than-ever))

### Risk Factors

- **Skiathlon recovery:** Diggins finished 8th in the skiathlon on Feb 7 (+2:21.1) after being caught in a first-lap crash caused by Nissinen. While she was uninjured, the physical and psychological toll of that incident -- and the disappointment of an 8th-place finish -- could linger. Alternatively, it could sharpen her motivation. ([NBC Olympics](https://www.nbcolympics.com/news/frida-karlsson-wins-gold-skiathlon-jessie-diggins-caught-lap-mishap))
- **Age and cumulative fatigue:** At 34, Diggins is carrying the weight of a full World Cup season plus the Tour de Ski. Three demanding races in the space of six days (skiathlon Feb 7, 10km free Feb 12) is taxing even for the fittest athletes.
- **Karlsson factor:** Frida Karlsson's dominant skiathlon gold (winning by 51 seconds) demonstrated a level of form that could challenge even Diggins in the freestyle distance.

### Prediction

**Expected finish: 1st-3rd.** Diggins is the most likely gold medalist in this race. The format, technique, distance, and her current form all align perfectly. If she skis her race cleanly and paces intelligently -- as she almost always does in interval starts -- she should be on the podium and very likely on top of it.

## 2. Frida Karlsson (SWE) -- BIB 42

### Why She's a Contender

Frida Karlsson arrives at this race as the reigning Olympic champion, having won the skiathlon gold just five days earlier with a devastating 51-second winning margin. That performance announced her as the most dangerous athlete in the field. When Karlsson is healthy and fully committed, she may possess the highest ceiling of any female cross-country skier currently racing.

**Current form:** Karlsson's season has been carefully managed around her recurring foot injury. She has been highly selective with her schedule, skipping Davos, withdrawing from the Tour de Ski (foot), and missing Oberhof and Goms entirely. But when she has raced, the results have been emphatic: she won the World Cup season opener at Ruka (10km classic) and then delivered the defining performance of these Games so far with her skiathlon gold. Post-race, she told HBO Max: "I felt like a machine. I've never enjoyed skiing as much as I did today." TV 2 consultant Petter Skinstad declared: "Frida Karlsson is the best skier in the world." ([Nordic Mag](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/2026-olympics-cross-country-skiing-i-felt-like-a-machine-says-olympic-champion-frida-karlsson-0207/), [NBC Olympics](https://www.nbcolympics.com/news/frida-karlsson-wins-gold-skiathlon-jessie-diggins-caught-lap-mishap))

**Technique fit:** Freestyle is arguably Karlsson's strongest technique. She pulled away from Ebba Andersson in the freestyle half of the skiathlon to build her 51-second margin. She holds two World Championship silver medals in the 10km freestyle (Oberstdorf 2021 and Planica 2023) and won the 50km freestyle at the 2025 Trondheim Worlds. The 10km interval start free is a natural fit for her skillset. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frida_Karlsson))

**Course suitability:** Karlsson is a superb climber. She won the Tour de Ski overall in 2023 (which concludes with the Alpe Cermis climb), won the 50km over hilly terrain at Trondheim, and dominated the demanding Tesero skiathlon course. The course's 390m of climbing rewards athletes with her combination of endurance and climbing power.

**Venue history:** Karlsson has raced at Val di Fiemme repeatedly through Tour de Ski stages and now the Olympic skiathlon. She won the Tour de Ski overall here in 2023 and won the Olympic skiathlon gold on this course just five days ago. She trained at altitude in nearby Lavaze in the weeks before the Games. Her familiarity with this terrain is excellent. ([ProXCskiing](https://www.proxcskiing.com/traditional-skiing/olympic-focus-sees-swedish-stars-withdraw-from-world-cup/))

**Head-to-head:** Karlsson's 51-second demolition of Andersson and the rest of the field in the skiathlon was the most dominant individual performance of the Games so far. She beat Diggins by over 2 minutes (though Diggins was compromised by the crash). At her best, Karlsson can beat anyone.

### Risk Factors

- **Foot injury:** The recurring foot problem has haunted Karlsson's season. She withdrew from the Tour de Ski in January due to foot pain and has managed the issue carefully. The skiathlon was demanding, and racing again five days later on a foot that has caused problems all season is a legitimate concern. Whether the foot can hold up for a second high-intensity Olympic race in the same week is the key question. ([Nordic Mag](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/cross-country-skiing-tour-de-ski-frida-karlsson-withdraws-from-the-alpe-cermis-climb-after-suffering-a-foot-injury-0104/))
- **Limited racing this season:** Karlsson has started only about nine races this season due to her selective schedule. While this preserved her body for the Olympics, it means she has less race sharpness than athletes like Diggins or Ilar who have raced consistently.
- **Interval start vs. mass start:** Karlsson's skiathlon gold came in a mass start format where she could use her aggressive front-running style to break opponents. The interval start format requires different skills -- self-pacing without the stimulus of direct competition. Her Trondheim 10km free interval start result (8th, +29.9s) shows she is not as dominant in this format as in mass starts.

### Prediction

**Expected finish: 1st-3rd.** Karlsson is the co-favorite alongside Diggins. If the foot holds, she has the form and the confidence to win a second Olympic gold. The risk of the foot injury is the only factor preventing her from being the outright favorite.

## 3. Ebba Andersson (SWE) -- BIB 40

### Why She's a Contender

Ebba Andersson is the most complete 10km interval start skier in this field based on career body of work. Seven of her nine World Cup victories have come at the 10km distance, and her most recent World Cup win (Trondheim, December 7, 2025) was a 10km interval start freestyle in 26:05.3. She is the reigning 10km World Champion (classic, Trondheim 2025) and the Olympic skiathlon silver medalist from just five days ago. The 10km interval start free format is, statistically speaking, her strongest event.

**Current form:** Andersson's unconventional Olympic preparation -- skipping all World Cup racing from January 4 onwards to train with her father at home in Solleftea -- paid dividends with her skiathlon silver. She finished the Tour de Ski 4th overall and won the Trondheim 10km free before going into her private training block. Her skiathlon silver (+51.0 behind Karlsson) confirmed her fitness, even if Karlsson was clearly superior on that day. ([FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/cross-country/news/2025-26/andersson-leads-swedish-party-in-skiathlon-at-milano-cortina))

**Technique fit:** Andersson is elite in freestyle. Her Trondheim 10km free victory (26:05.3) was dominant, and the freestyle half of her skiathlon silver was strong. She has won across both classic and freestyle, making her one of the most versatile distance skiers in the sport. ([NBC Olympics](https://www.nbcolympics.com/news/ebba-andersson-earns-first-world-cup-victory-season-10km-free-jessie-diggins-clinches-3rd))

**Course suitability:** Andersson is an outstanding climber. She has multiple top-5 Tour de Ski overall finishes built on climbing strength, and her athletics background (3000m track runner) gives her an exceptional aerobic engine. The 390m of climbing on this course suits her endurance profile perfectly. ([FasterSkier](https://fasterskier.com/2025/11/sundling-sprints-to-20-k-victory-diggins-second/))

**Format advantage:** The interval start format is Andersson's strongest format. Seven of her nine World Cup wins came in interval start races. The format removes the sprint-finish weakness that sometimes costs her in mass starts. She can set her own pace and race the clock, which is exactly how she prefers to compete. ([FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/cross-country/news/2025-26/andersson-makes-it-a-2025-trondheim-treble-with-first-win-of-world-cup-season))

**Motivation:** The skiathlon silver was Andersson's first individual Olympic medal after three Games. Before the Olympics, she stated: "I don't have an individual Olympic medal yet, so that is something I really want to bring home from Italy." She delivered on that ambition with silver -- but she will now be hungry for gold. ([FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/cross-country/news/2025-26/andersson-leads-swedish-party-in-skiathlon-at-milano-cortina))

### Risk Factors

- **51-second gap to Karlsson:** The skiathlon gap suggests Karlsson is operating at a higher level right now. Andersson will need to close a significant performance gap to challenge her teammate for gold.
- **Month-long racing absence:** Andersson did not race competitively from January 4 to February 7. While the skiathlon showed this approach can work, the lack of competitive intensity over five weeks could affect her sharpness in a pure time-trial format.
- **Fatigue from skiathlon:** The 20km skiathlon was demanding. Recovery across five days for a second 10km race at Olympic intensity is a challenge.

### Prediction

**Expected finish: 1st-4th.** Andersson is a clear podium contender. The 10km interval start free is arguably her strongest event in all of cross-country skiing. She has the pedigree, the recent form, and the format-specific excellence to medal. Gold is possible but may depend on whether Diggins and Karlsson have off days.

## 4. Heidi Weng (NOR) -- BIB 32

### Why She's a Contender

Heidi Weng has been one of the most consistent performers of the 2025-26 season. She earned four podiums in her first five World Cup distance races, finished 3rd overall at the Tour de Ski, and then claimed Olympic skiathlon bronze on Feb 7 -- her second Olympic medal, twelve years after her first. At 34, she is experiencing a remarkable late-career resurgence after overcoming a devastating concussion, COVID-19, and vertigo.

**Current form:** Weng's season has been outstanding. She opened with back-to-back podiums at Ruka (3rd in 10km classic, 3rd in 20km free), took 2nd in the Trondheim skiathlon (just 0.46s off the win), and finished 3rd overall at the Tour de Ski. She strategically skipped Oberhof and Goms to prepare for the Olympics, and the gamble paid off with skiathlon bronze. Her freestyle strength was on full display in the skiathlon -- she was 8th at the ski change and surged through the freestyle section to catch and pass Astrid Slind for bronze. ([Nordic Mag](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/2026-olympic-games-cross-country-skiing-its-incredible-heidi-weng-wins-olympic-medal-again-twelve-years-on-0207/))

**Technique fit:** Weng's freestyle ability is excellent and may be her stronger technique, as her skiathlon bronzes in both 2014 and 2026 were built on freestyle surges. Her 3rd-place finish in the Tour de Ski final stage at Val di Fiemme (a freestyle mass start) confirms her current skating form.

**Course suitability:** Weng holds the fastest recorded time for the Alpe Cermis final climb at Val di Fiemme (18:02.1, set in 2018). Her fell running background gives her extraordinary uphill power. With 390m of total climbing, this course rewards her climbing strength. ([Fiemme World Cup](https://www.fiemmeworldcup.com/en/news/news-list/the-queen-of-tour-de-ski-final-climb))

**Venue history:** Weng has an outstanding record at Val di Fiemme: 2013 World Championship skiathlon bronze and relay gold, 2019 Tour de Ski 10km freestyle mass start win, 2025 Tour de Ski podium, and 2026 Olympic skiathlon bronze. This venue brings out her best. ([FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/cross-country/news/2018-19/heidi-weng-takes-revenge-on-oestberg-in-val-di-fiemme-mst-highlights-miqzihroem0))

**Head-to-head:** In the skiathlon, Weng was 1:26.7 behind gold medalist Karlsson and 35.7 seconds behind silver medalist Andersson. In the Trondheim 10km free interval start, she finished 10th. In freestyle distance events more broadly this season, she has been consistently inside the top five.

### Risk Factors

- **10km interval start is not her optimal format:** Weng's greatest strengths show in mass start racing, skiathlons, and stage races where she can use tactical positioning. Her 10th-place finish in the Trondheim 10km free interval start suggests the pure time-trial format is less favorable than mass starts.
- **Post-concussion considerations:** While Weng has recovered remarkably, the long-term effects of her 2022 concussion and subsequent vertigo (BPPV diagnosis in 2023) remain part of her story. ([ProXCskiing](https://www.proxcskiing.com/traditional-skiing/heidi-weng-i-thought-it-was-all-over-then-came-the-turning-point/))
- **Sprint weakness:** Her inability to produce top-end speed (52nd in Tour de Ski sprint qualification) means she must build her advantage over the course of the race rather than producing an explosive effort on climbs.

### Prediction

**Expected finish: 3rd-6th.** Weng is a strong medal contender but faces a format disadvantage in the interval start. Her venue history and freestyle strength make a podium realistic, though she may need one of the top three favorites to falter. She is the leading "podium-or-just-outside" candidate.

## 5. Teresa Stadlober (AUT) -- BIB 50

### Why She's a Contender

Teresa Stadlober is having the best season of her career at age 33. She finished 2nd overall in the Tour de Ski (winning the Climber's Bib), sits 4th in the World Cup overall standings (811 pts), and has earned four World Cup podiums including a near-win at Oberhof (lost by just 0.7 seconds). Her Tour de Ski performance -- which included a 2nd-place finish in the Val di Fiemme freestyle mass start stage -- demonstrated world-class form on this exact terrain just five weeks ago. She is the most improved athlete in the field and the one with the most dramatic upward trajectory.

**Current form:** Stadlober's consistency has been extraordinary: 2nd at Tour de Ski Toblach classic, 2nd at Toblach 5km free, 3rd at Toblach 20km pursuit, 2nd at Val di Fiemme 10km free mass start, and 2nd at Oberhof 10km classic. She has the highest prize money earnings (69,300 CHF) of any woman on the 2025-26 World Cup circuit. Her Tour de Ski runner-up finish demonstrated she can sustain high performance over multiple demanding races. ([FasterSkier](https://fasterskier.com/2026/01/one-final-climb-diggins-victorious-in-tour-de-ski/), [ProXCskiing](https://www.proxcskiing.com/traditional-skiing/cross-country-world-cup-2025-2026-updated-standings-after-oberhof/))

**Technique fit:** While Stadlober's classic technique is her strongest, she has proven she can compete at the highest level in freestyle. Her 2nd-place finish in the Tour de Ski Val di Fiemme freestyle mass start stage -- on this same course -- is direct evidence. Her Olympic bronze came in the skiathlon at Beijing (which includes a freestyle half). She is not a pure freestyle specialist, but she is a legitimate contender in freestyle distance races.

**Course suitability:** Stadlober won the Tour de Ski Climber's Bib (45 points), recognizing her as the best climber in the multi-stage competition. On a course with 390m of total climbing, this distinction matters enormously. She thrives on demanding, hilly terrain. ([FasterSkier](https://fasterskier.com/2026/01/one-final-climb-diggins-victorious-in-tour-de-ski/))

**Venue history:** Stadlober raced at Val di Fiemme during the Tour de Ski in January 2026, finishing 2nd in the 10km freestyle mass start stage and 4th on the Alpe Cermis final climb. She knows this course well from multiple Tour de Ski editions. ([Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/milano-cortina-2026/news/2025-26-tour-de-ski-full-schedule-all-results-standings-complete-list/))

**Redemption narrative:** Stadlober's career has one of the great redemption arcs in Olympic skiing. At PyeongChang 2018, she was running 2nd in the 30km with 7km to go when she took a wrong turn, finishing 9th. She redeemed herself with Olympic bronze at Beijing 2022. Now, in the form of her life, she could cap that arc with a second Olympic medal or even her first World Cup victory. ([ESPN](http://www.espn.com/olympics/story/_/id/22574076))

### Risk Factors

- **Arrived with a cold:** Stadlober traveled to the Olympics while fighting illness, which affected her skiathlon (9th place, below her season form). Whether she has fully recovered in the five days since the skiathlon is a critical question. She indicated after the skiathlon that the skating portion went well and expressed confidence for the 10km free. ([ORF Sport](https://sport.orf.at/milanocortina2026/stories/3151161/))
- **No World Cup victories:** Despite nine career podiums and a season at her best, Stadlober has never won a World Cup race. Whether she can break through to win at the Olympic level, against Diggins and Karlsson, is a psychological barrier she has not yet overcome.
- **Freestyle vs. classic:** Her best results tend to come in classic, and her 9th-place skiathlon was not the marker of a gold medal contender. She will need a step up from that result to reach the podium.
- **Racing alone:** As Austria's sole top-level woman, Stadlober does not benefit from the team support, pacing data, or wax-sharing advantages that the deep Swedish and Norwegian squads enjoy.

### Prediction

**Expected finish: 4th-8th.** Stadlober is the most dangerous outside contender in this field. On her best day, with a favorable course and a healthy body, she can podium. A medal would require her to overcome the illness she carried into the Games and to perform at or above her Tour de Ski best. She is the kind of athlete who elevates at championships -- her Beijing bronze proved that.

She was 2nd in the 10km free at both Trondheim and Davos this season. However, her disappointing 14th place in the skiathlon (+3:41.0) is a significant concern and suggests she may struggle on this course. If she bounces back, she is a medal threat. If the skiathlon was representative, she may land in the 6th-10th range. ([ProXCskiing](https://www.proxcskiing.com/traditional-skiing/a-swedish-breakthrough-rising-star-sends-a-clear-olympic-message/))

She beat Diggins by 8.8 seconds on the Alpe Cermis climb. However, her 13th-place skiathlon was mediocre, and she has limited experience at the highest level of competition. A medal is possible but more likely to be in the 5th-10th range. ([FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/cross-country/news/2025-26/simpson-larsen-in-shock-after-first-world-cup-victory))

**Nadja Kaelin (SUI) -- BIB 56:** The Swiss 24-year-old stunned with a 4th-place skiathlon finish, just 25 seconds from a medal -- the best Swiss women's individual Olympic XC result since 1988. Her freestyle surge in the skiathlon's second half was remarkable. Whether she can replicate that over a pure 10km freestyle interval start is uncertain, but she clearly has the form for a surprise. ([blue News](https://www.bluewin.ch/en/sport/nadja-kaelin-brilliant-fourth-in-the-skiathlon-3085321.html))

**Kerttu Niskanen (FIN) -- BIB 60:** An Olympic silver medalist in the 10km classic at Beijing 2022 (lost by just 0.4s to Johaug), Niskanen placed 5th in the skiathlon and has been trending upward all season (4th in Goms 20km). At 37, she is a classic specialist who has improved dramatically in freestyle. Her late start position (BIB 60) is not ideal. A top-10 is more likely than a podium, but she should not be dismissed entirely. ([Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/athletes/kerttu-niskanen))

## Summary

### Predicted Podium

| **GOLD** | Jessie Diggins | USA | High |
| **SILVER** | Frida Karlsson | SWE | Medium-High |
| **BRONZE** | Ebba Andersson | SWE | Medium |

### Analysis

The 10km interval start freestyle is the event most perfectly tailored to Jessie Diggins' strengths. She is the World Cup leader, the reigning world champion in this discipline, a three-time Tour de Ski champion, and she carries the motivation of a farewell Olympic campaign chasing an individual gold. Her late start (BIB 44) gives her split-time intelligence on the entire field. Barring a physical setback from the skiathlon crash or the cumulative toll of the season, Diggins should be the gold standard.

Frida Karlsson's skiathlon dominance makes her the clear co-favorite, but the foot injury that has plagued her all season, combined with the different demands of the interval start format compared to her preferred mass start racing, introduces enough uncertainty to place her as the primary threat rather than the outright favorite. If healthy, she can win.

Ebba Andersson's statistical dominance in the 10km interval start format (seven of nine World Cup wins at this distance and format) makes her the most dangerous lurking contender. In a time-trial format where she can ski her own race without sprint-finish vulnerability, Andersson is capable of beating anyone on any given day.

Heidi Weng is the wild card with the venue history, freestyle surge capacity, and championship pedigree to reach the podium, but the interval start format is not her optimal environment. Teresa Stadlober is the most improved athlete in the field and the best climber outside the top three, but her illness and the absence of a World Cup victory suggest she is more likely to finish just off the podium.

The depth of the field means that any result from 1st through 8th is plausible for a half-dozen athletes. This is one of the most open women's distance races in Olympic history, but the top three -- Diggins, Karlsson, and Andersson -- have separated themselves from the rest of the field this season and should be favored to occupy the medal positions.
