# Technique Fit Matrix: Women's 10km Interval Start Free

**Event:** Olympic Winter Games Milano Cortina 2026
**Venue:** Tesero Cross-Country Skiing Stadium, Val di Fiemme, Italy
**Date:** THU 12 FEB 2026 | 13:00 CET

---

## Race Demands Profile

| Parameter | Detail | Implication |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Distance** | 10km (~9,961m actual) | Favors middle-distance specialists; too short for pure marathon types, too long for sprint specialists |
| **Technique** | Freestyle (skating) | Demands elite V2/V1 skating technique; classic specialists at disadvantage |
| **Format** | Interval start (30s gaps) | Pure time trial -- rewards self-pacing, eliminates pack tactics and sprint finishes |
| **Total Climb** | 390m (3 laps) | Heavily climbing course -- demands exceptional uphill power and lactate management |
| **Altitude** | ~960m | Moderate altitude; minor advantage for altitude-trained athletes |
| **Lap Structure** | 3 laps x ~3.3km | Repeated climbing efforts; pacing discipline is critical |
| **Course Character** | Technical descents, long climbs, stadium finish | Rewards complete skiers who combine climbing power with technical downhill skill |

### Key Demands Summary
The ideal athlete for this race possesses: (1) elite freestyle technique, (2) exceptional climbing power for 3 repeated ascents, (3) strong interval start/time-trial pacing experience, (4) proven 10km distance fitness, (5) familiarity with the Tesero course and altitude, and (6) peak current form entering the race.

## Key Tactical Observations

### Athletes Whose Profiles Best Match This Race

1. **Ebba Andersson** has the single most relevant result: she won a 10km Interval Start Free at Trondheim (Dec 7, 2025) in 26:05.3. Seven of her nine career WC wins have come at 10km distance. This is her format.

2. **Karoline Simpson-Larsen** won both a 10km Free IS (Davos) and the Tour de Ski Final Climb at this venue this season. Her combined freestyle, climbing, IS, and venue ratings make her the strongest composite fit -- if she can replicate her best form after a disappointing skiathlon.

3. **Jessie Diggins** combines the most well-rounded skill set: elite freestyle, elite climbing, elite IS pacing, and the strategic advantage of starting with BIB 44 (complete split-time information). Her only weakness is venue-specific -- she was 8th in the skiathlon here, but that was crash-affected.

### Athletes at Greatest Risk of Underperformance

1. **Astrid Oeyre Slind** -- Her classic-freestyle gap was exposed in the skiathlon. If she cannot find better freestyle speed than five days ago, the 10km Free will magnify that weakness over a full race at skating technique.

2. **Delphine Claudel** -- Despite being arguably the most successful athlete at this specific venue (WC win, 4 Alpe Cermis podiums), her fitness after bone oedema is severely compromised. Her 31st-place skiathlon showed she is not close to her best level.

3. **Emma Ribom** -- A sprinter stretching into distance territory on a heavy climbing course with no venue history. The 390m of total climb will test her endurance limits.

### Potential Overperformers

1. **Kerttu Niskanen** -- Her freestyle has improved dramatically in recent seasons (first freestyle podiums in 2025 at age 36). Her 5th-place skiathlon, upward season trajectory, and climbing ability suggest she could outperform her "C" freestyle rating.

2. **Krista Parmakoski** -- Her 12 championship medals and history of peaking at major events mean her "D" form rating may not reflect her Olympic day performance. She won Olympic bronze in 10km freestyle at PyeongChang 2018 on a similar climbing course.

3. **Heidi Weng** -- Her freestyle surge from 8th to 3rd in the skiathlon showed she is in exceptional skating form. The Val di Fiemme venue is her spiritual home (2013 WC gold, 2019 TdS win, Cermis record holder). She could surprise with a medal.
