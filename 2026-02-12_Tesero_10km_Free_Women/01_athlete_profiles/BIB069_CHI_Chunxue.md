## Personal Life & Background
Chi Chunxue (Chinese: 池春雪; pinyin: Chi Chunxue) was born on January 4, 1998, in Jixi, Heilongjiang Province, in northeastern China ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chi_Chunxue)). She stands 163 cm (5'4") tall ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chi_Chunxue)). Heilongjiang Province has long been the heartland of China's cross-country skiing development, and Chi is one of the most experienced products of that system.

Chi began her international career at a young age, representing China as the sole cross-country skiing athlete at the 2016 Youth Olympic Games in Lillehammer, Norway, where she secured a place in the quarterfinals of the women's short-distance classic with a time of 3:40.94 and ranked ninth in the freestyle event ([Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/athletes/chunxue-chi)). She won the women's 5km at the 2014/15 FIS Junior World Ski Championships, becoming one of the first Chinese women to win an FIS cross-country event ([The-Sports.org](https://www.the-sports.org/chunxue-chi-cross-country-skiing-spf323759.html)).

Chi has competed across a remarkably wide range of disciplines: 10km individual, 10km mass start, 10km pursuit, 15km, 15km pursuit, 30km, 5km, skiathlon, sprint, relay, and team sprint ([Olympics.com](https://www.olympics.com/en/athletes/chunxue-chi)). She is also the Vasaloppet China 32km champion, demonstrating her ability in marathon-format events ([Vasaloppet China](https://www.vasaloppetchina.com/congratulations-to-wang-qiang-and-chi-chunxue-to-won-the-champion-of-vasaloppet-china-32km/)). At 28, she is competing in her third consecutive Winter Olympics (PyeongChang 2018, Beijing 2022, Milano Cortina 2026), making her one of the most Games-experienced athletes in this field ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chi_Chunxue)).
