## Personal Life & Background

### Hometown & Val di Fiemme Roots
Caterina Ganz was born on 13 November 1995 and grew up in the Val di Fiemme valley in Trentino, Italy. She currently resides in Moena, a town in the adjacent Val di Fassa. The Tesero Cross-Country Skiing Stadium -- the site of the Olympic 10km -- is just a short drive from where she lives. She has described the surreal feeling of racing at a home Olympics: *"It's a little bit strange to have the Olympics at home because you always think that they'd be so far away -- in China or America. Now the Olympic course is 10 minutes from my home."* ([FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/cross-country/news/2024-25/italy-s-stars-who-will-have-the-olympic-cross-country-on-their-doorstep-we-will-also-use-the-home-pressure-to-go-faster-)) ([FIS Athlete Biography](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sectorcode=cc&competitorid=175848&type=result))

### Family
Ganz's father is an architect and her mother is a shopkeeper. Her passion for cross-country skiing was born by chance and was fostered by her enrollment at the Ski College in Pozza di Fassa (Liceo Artistico), which allowed her to train consistently and develop her skills, thanks to the guidance of coach Attilio Dellagiacoma. ([CONI - Pechino 2022](https://pechino2022.coni.it/italia-team/atleta/1770-Caterina_GANZ.html))

### Education
She attended the Liceo Artistico (Art High School) attached to the Ski College in Pozza di Fassa, combining artistic education with intensive ski training -- an unusual educational path for a cross-country skier. ([CONI - Pechino 2022](https://pechino2022.coni.it/italia-team/atleta/1770-Caterina_GANZ.html))

### Fiamme Gialle -- Military Sports Group
As a member of the Guardia di Finanza (Italian Financial Police) sports group known as Fiamme Gialle, Ganz has institutional support for her athletic career. This is a common arrangement for Italian national team athletes, providing stable employment and training infrastructure while competing at the highest level. ([Fiamme Gialle](http://www.fiammegialle.org/index.php?Itemid=278&id=1400&option=com_k2&view=item))

### Team Spirit & Humility
Ganz has shown a humble and team-oriented mentality throughout her career. She has acknowledged the strength of other nations while expressing pride in Italy's improvement: *"We know that we are not the talent that Sundling and them have, but we have a lot of strong girls in the team."* This self-awareness and team-first attitude has made her a valued member of the Italian squad for relays and team sprints. ([FIS](https://www.fis-ski.com/cross-country/news/2024-25/italy-s-stars-who-will-have-the-olympic-cross-country-on-their-doorstep-we-will-also-use-the-home-pressure-to-go-faster-))

### Val di Fiemme's Skiing Heritage
Ganz follows in the footsteps of Val di Fiemme skiing legends Franco Nones (1968 Olympic 30km gold from nearby Castello di Fiemme) and Giorgio Vanzetta (4 Olympic medals from Val di Fiemme). The valley has a deep tradition in Nordic skiing that dates back generations. ([visitfiemme.it](https://www.visitfiemme.it/en/events/winter-olympics))
