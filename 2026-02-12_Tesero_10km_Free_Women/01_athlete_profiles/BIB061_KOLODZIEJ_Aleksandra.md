## Personal Life & Background
Aleksandra Kolodziej hails from Letowe, a small village in the municipality of Mszana Dolna in the Lesser Poland (Malopolska) region of southern Poland ([Sadeckie.pl](https://xn--sdeckie-p4a.pl/artykul/aleksandra-kolodziej-nowa-olimpijka-z-gminy-mszana-dolna)). She competes for the LKS Witow Ski club based in nearby Mszana Gorna ([FIS Athlete Biography](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sectorcode=cc&competitorid=250720)). In the history of the Mszana Dolna municipality, six people have competed in the Olympics, including five from the nearby village of Kasina Wielka and one from Lubomierz; among them, five Olympic medals have been won, including two golds -- all earned by cross-country skiing legend Justyna Kowalczyk ([Sadeckie.pl](https://xn--sdeckie-p4a.pl/artykul/aleksandra-kolodziej-nowa-olimpijka-z-gminy-mszana-dolna)). Kolodziej is the newest Olympian from this proud skiing community and will celebrate her 22nd birthday on February 14 during these Olympic Games ([Sadeckie.pl](https://xn--sdeckie-p4a.pl/artykul/aleksandra-kolodziej-nowa-olimpijka-z-gminy-mszana-dolna)). She was part of Poland's cross-country skiing development program, progressing through the junior national team ranks at Junior World Championships (2022-2024) before earning her senior national team spot ([FIS Athlete Biography](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sectorcode=cc&competitorid=250720)).

## Key Storylines
- At just 21 years old (turning 22 during the Games on February 14), Kolodziej represents the future of Polish cross-country skiing and is making her Olympic debut.
- She was part of a historic Polish Tour de Ski contingent -- seven Polish athletes completed the 2025-26 Tour de Ski, the most in the nation's history. Coach Olof Hedberg praised the "depth and quality of performances."
- Poland has a proud cross-country skiing tradition anchored by Justyna Kowalczyk, who won the Tour de Ski four times. Kolodziej is part of the new generation carrying that legacy forward.
- Her FIS points have improved dramatically through the 2025-26 season, dropping from 132.27 to 100.23 in distance -- a sign of rapid development and increasing competitiveness.
- She earned her Olympic spot through World Cup points accumulated during the qualification period, prevailing over other Polish candidates.
- Her teammate Eliza Rucka-Michalek achieved a career-best 6th place at a World Cup race during the Tour de Ski, showing the Polish women's program is on the rise.
