## Personal Life & Background
Kristrun Gudnadottir (Icelandic: Kristrun Gudnadottir) was born on 13 October 1997 and represents Iceland in cross-country skiing ([Olympics.com — Kristrun Gudnadottir](https://olympics.com/en/athletes/kristrun-gudnadottir)). She is one of Iceland's only active international cross-country skiers, representing a volcanic island nation of approximately 380,000 people that has never won a Winter Olympic medal despite competing since 1948 ([Olympics.com — Iceland](https://www.olympics.com/ioc/iceland)). She was honored as **Iceland's flag bearer at the Beijing 2022 Opening Ceremony** alongside alpine skier Sturla Snaer Snorrason, a recognition of her pioneering role in Icelandic Nordic sport ([Olympics Wiki — Kristrun Gudnadottir](https://olympics.fandom.com/wiki/Kristr%C3%BAn_Gu%C3%B0nad%C3%B3ttir)). Iceland qualified one female cross-country skier for Milano Cortina 2026 through the FIS basic quota system ([Iceland at the 2026 Winter Olympics — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iceland_at_the_2026_Winter_Olympics)). Gudnadottir achieved a notable 15th place finish in sprint at the 2024 Scandinavian Cup, her best international result ([the-sports.org — Kristrun Gudnadottir](https://www.the-sports.org/kristrun-gudnadottir-cross-country-skiing-spf576237.html)).
