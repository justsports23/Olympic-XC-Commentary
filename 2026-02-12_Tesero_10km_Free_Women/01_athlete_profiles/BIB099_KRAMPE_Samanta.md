## Personal Life & Background
Samanta Krampe is a 22-year-old Latvian cross-country skier who competes for the club **SS Arkadija** ([FIS Athlete Biography](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sectorcode=cc&competitorid=235050)). She debuted at the Olympics at just 19 in Beijing 2022, where she was part of the Latvian women's relay team ([Olympedia — Samanta Krampe](https://www.olympedia.org/athletes/139045)). She is part of Latvia's biggest-ever Winter Olympic delegation for Milano Cortina 2026, alongside teammates Linda Kaparkaleja (BIB 96) and veteran Kitija Auzina, as well as Patricija Eiduka on the men's side ([LSM.lv — Biggest-ever Latvian team](https://eng.lsm.lv/article/culture/sport/20.01.2026-biggest-ever-latvian-winter-olympic-team-picked.a630669/)). Krampe and Kaparkaleja have a close competitive partnership, having won a Junior Roller Ski World Championship team event together in Madona in 2022 ([FIS — Madona World Cup](https://www.fis-ski.com/en/cross-country/cross-country-news-multimedia/news/2022-23/madona-world-cup-and-junior-world-championships)). ## Key Storylines
Krampe is making her second Olympic appearance after debuting at Beijing 2022 at age 19. She is one of three Latvian women in cross-country skiing at these Games alongside teammate Linda Kaparkaleja (BIB 96) and Kitija Auzina. A dominant force on the Latvian domestic scene with her commanding Latloppet marathon victory, she also has roller skiing pedigree, winning a Junior World Championship team event with Kaparkaleja. The two young Latvians form a strong partnership for the future of Latvian cross-country skiing.
