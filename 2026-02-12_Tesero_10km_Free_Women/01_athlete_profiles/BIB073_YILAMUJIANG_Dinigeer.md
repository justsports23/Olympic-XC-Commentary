## Personal Life & Background

### Uyghur Heritage & Hometown
Dinigeer Yilamujiang was born on 3 May 2001 in Altay City, Altay Prefecture, in the far-northwestern corner of the Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region, bordering Kazakhstan, Russia, and Mongolia. She is of Uyghur ethnicity, making her one of the most internationally visible Uyghur athletes in the world. [Wikipedia - Dinigeer Yilamujiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dinigeer_Yilamujiang) [MEAWW](https://meaww.com/who-is-dinigeer-yilamujiang-the-uyghur-athlete-at-the-forefront-of-beijings-sportwashing-scandal)

Her hometown of Altay is widely recognized as one of the earliest known origins of skiing -- a rock painting depicting people hunting on skis was discovered near Altay in 2005 and is estimated to be over 10,000 years old. ### How She Started Skiing
Dinigeer was skiing by age 5 as a way of getting around her snowy hometown. In 2009, the Altay government sought to revive skiing in the region. In early 2010, her father was appointed to coach a cross-country skiing team of 20 teenagers. "They all started from zero. There were three girls in the team, and Dinigeer was one of them," her father recalled. The team could not afford to rent a snow groomer, so father and young skiers made the grooves themselves -- standing in line abreast in their skis and moving laterally to press down a 3km trail. "When the snow was heavy, or more than 1m deep, it would take us seven or eight hours to press the snow," he said. "Since the piste needs to be hardened during the night, we could only practice the next day." [China Daily](https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202202/06/WS61ffd371a310cdd39bc84eee.html) [Fudan University](https://www.fudan.edu.cn/en/2022/0209/c344a130127/page.htm)

She originally also demonstrated talent as a track and field athlete, competing as a distance runner in high school. Despite her cross-country skiing experience with the youth team, Dinigeer did not turn professional until 2017, when she was among a dozen youngsters selected from 170 hopefuls in Xinjiang as part of a cross-sports development program. [San Diego Union-Tribune](https://www.sandiegouniontribune.com/news/nation-world/story/2022-02-05/what-message-did-china-send-by-choosing-uyghur-torchbearer) [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dilnigar_Ilhamjan)

### Training in Norway
Norwegian coach Kristian Bjune Sveen travelled to Xinjiang to give her training, and Dinigeer also spent approximately three years training in Norway herself, alongside Bayani Jialin, a Chinese skier of Kazakh ethnicity. This European training was part of China's massive investment program in cross-country skiing ahead of the 2022 Beijing Olympics. ### The Beijing 2022 Cauldron Moment
At the Opening Ceremony on 4 February 2022, the 20-year-old Yilamujiang and Nordic combined skier Zhao Jiawen were the final torchbearers. They carried the Olympic torch into the National Stadium (Bird's Nest), were lifted onto a platform, and jointly placed the flame into the center of a giant snowflake-shaped cauldron. She became the first person of Uyghur ethnicity and the first native of Altay to light the Olympic cauldron. [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/olympics/2022/02/05/dinigeer-yilamujiang-cauldron-olympic-skiing/) [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2022/02/04/1078234213/beijing-olympics-flame-torch-uyghur)

China's selection of a Uyghur athlete for this highly symbolic role sparked intense international debate. Human rights organizations and Western governments -- many of which had announced diplomatic boycotts of the Games over China's treatment of Uyghurs in Xinjiang -- accused Beijing of using Yilamujiang as a propaganda tool. The IOC stated that her ethnicity was not a factor in the decision and that as a participating athlete, she had the right to take part in any ceremony. [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2022/2/5/beijing-winter-olympics-china-uighur-torchbearer) [Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/2022/02/05/beijing-olympics-china-s-choice-of-uyghur-torchbearer-causes-controversy)

After her skiathlon race, she exited through a separate aisle, without passing through the mixed zone where she could be interviewed by the press. Unnamed sources claimed she was physically and mentally exhausted from "having the eyes of the world on her." She was scheduled to race in the women's 4x5km relay on 12 February but never showed up. [Infobae/AroundTheRings](https://www.infobae.com/aroundtherings/articles/2022/02/05/dinigeer-yilamujiang-makes-headlines-as-a-symbol-not-as-a-skier/)
