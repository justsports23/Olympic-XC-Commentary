## Personal Life & Background
- **Hometown:** Winthrop, Washington, a small mountain community in the Methow Valley of the North Cascades ([The Seattle Times](https://www.seattletimes.com/sports/olympics/winthrops-novie-mccabe-continues-impressive-rise-with-24th-place-olympic-cross-country-finish/))
- **Father -- Sean McCabe:** Novie's father was a teacher, artist, skier, and climber. He passed away on 7 November 2009 from thyroid cancer at age 46, when Novie was just seven years old. His artwork regularly appeared in publications such as the Alpinist, Cross Country Skier, Climbing, and the American Alpine Journal. ([KXLY News](https://www.kxly.com/news/local-news/olympian-cross-country-skier-from-winthrop-follows-in-mother-s-footsteps/article_b39cc9d6-40b9-51e5-8bbf-6b97f3044af1.html))
- **Mother -- Laura McCabe:** A two-time Olympian (Lillehammer 1994, Nagano 1998) and long-distance specialist who won several marathon events in the US, including the American Birkebeiner. After Sean's passing, Laura raised Novie and her younger sister as a single mother while coaching with the Methow Valley Nordic Team. Laura once said: "Raising my two girls as a single mom, this community was hugely impactful and supportive." ([KXLY News](https://www.kxly.com/news/local-news/olympian-cross-country-skier-from-winthrop-follows-in-mother-s-footsteps/article_b39cc9d6-40b9-51e5-8bbf-6b97f3044af1.html))
- **Sister -- Dashe McCabe:** Novie's younger sister (born ~2006) is currently studying and skiing competitively at the University of Alaska-Anchorage. ([Team USA Profile](https://www.teamusa.com/profiles/novie-mccabe))
- **Her name:** "Novie is my real name, but it is not a family name or anything. My mom told me that she and my dad heard it on NPR and liked it. There is no other connection, other than that." ([Team USA Profile](https://www.teamusa.com/profiles/novie-mccabe))
- **Childhood dream:** In third grade, McCabe wrote that she wanted to ski in the Olympics, a goal she pursued relentlessly. She started racing competitively at age 14. ([Methow Valley News](https://methowvalleynews.com/2026/01/29/mccabe-headed-back-to-winter-olympics/))
- **Education:** Graduated from Liberty Bell High School in Winthrop, where she led the team to three straight WIAA state championships (2016-2018). Attended the University of Utah on an athletic scholarship and became a 3x NCAA Individual Champion. Now trains with the Alaska Pacific University Nordic Ski Center in Anchorage. ([University of Utah Athletics](https://utahutes.com/sports/alpine-skiing/roster/novie-mccabe/13483), [Deseret News](https://www.deseret.com/2023/3/7/23624400/utah-utes-head-to-ncaa-skiing-championships-lake-placid-novie-mccabe/))
- **Partner:** Luke Jager, a fellow cross-country skier who was also on the 2022 Beijing Olympic team. Their relationship grew from their time as University of Utah teammates and continued through the APU program. ([Team USA Profile](https://www.teamusa.com/profiles/novie-mccabe))
- **Idol:** Swedish cross-country skier Charlotte Kalla ([Team USA Profile](https://www.teamusa.com/profiles/novie-mccabe))
- **Hobbies:** Camping, backpacking, and exploring the outdoors ([Team USA Profile](https://www.teamusa.com/profiles/novie-mccabe))

## Career Highlights
- **2026 Winter Olympics (Milano Cortina):** 26th in women's skiathlon (10+10 km C/F) — showed an impressive skate section to move up through the field ([US Ski & Snowboard](https://www.usskiandsnowboard.org/news/diggins-eighth-olympic-skiathlon))
- **2022 Winter Olympics (Beijing):** 24th in 10 km Classic, 18th in 30 km Freestyle mass start, 6th in 4x5 km Relay ([The Seattle Times](https://www.seattletimes.com/sports/olympics/winthrops-novie-mccabe-continues-impressive-rise-with-24th-place-olympic-cross-country-finish/))
- **World Cup best result:** 7th in the Tour de Ski Final Climb (10 km mass start, Val di Fiemme, January 2022) ([US Ski & Snowboard](https://www.usskiandsnowboard.org/athletes/novie-mccabe))
- **World Cup career-best distance:** 11th in the 20 km skate in Falun, Sweden (2023-24 season) ([Team USA](https://www.teamusa.com/profiles/novie-mccabe-1228575))
- **NCAA:** 3x NCAA Individual Champion — swept 2023 NCAA Championships in Lake Placid (5 km freestyle and 20 km classic), won 5 km classic at Soldier Hollow (2022) ([University of Utah Athletics](https://utahutes.com/sports/alpine-skiing/roster/novie-mccabe/13483))
- **Junior Worlds:** Silver medal in relay at 2020 Junior World Championships in Oberwiesenthal — highest U.S. relay finish since the event was added to the championship schedule in 2001; 9th individually in 15 km mass start ([US Ski & Snowboard](https://www.usskiandsnowboard.org/athletes/novie-mccabe))
- **2025 U.S. National Champion:** Classic sprint ([Team USA](https://www.teamusa.com/profiles/novie-mccabe-1228575))
- **Gold Rush Award winner** (2023) — given to the Nordic Olympic woman who has demonstrated outstanding qualities of grit and grace ([FasterSkier](https://fasterskier.com/2023/06/novie-mccabe-gold-rush-winner-and-alaska-bound/))

## Key Storylines
- **Comeback season:** McCabe had a challenging 2024-25 season, starting only one race due to health issues. Her FIS status was listed as "Active (injured)" with injury status protection preserving her ranking points. She has bounced back strongly in 2025-26, returning to World Cup competition and earning Olympic selection. ([FIS Profile](https://www.fis-ski.com/DB/general/athlete-biography.html?sectorcode=cc&competitorid=208825&type=result))
- **Olympic legacy:** Her mother Laura McCabe is a two-time Olympian (Lillehammer 1994, Nagano 1998). Novie is now competing at her second Olympics, carrying on the family tradition. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Novie_McCabe))
- **Freestyle specialist in a freestyle race:** The 10 km free interval start format plays to McCabe's skating strengths. She demonstrated her strong freestyle ability in the Olympic skiathlon, moving up substantially during the skating portion. ([US Ski & Snowboard](https://www.usskiandsnowboard.org/news/diggins-eighth-olympic-skiathlon))
- **B-Team to Olympic starter:** Despite being placed on the B-Team for 2025-26, McCabe has earned her way onto the Olympic start list and is looking to improve on her skiathlon result. ([FasterSkier](https://fasterskier.com/2025/10/21-athletes-named-to-2025-26-stifel-u-s-cross-country-ski-team-ahead-of-olympic-season/))
