## Personal Life & Background
- **Hometown:** Sydney, Australia. She grew up in Sydney and now splits her time between Sydney and Norway [Australian Olympic Committee — Phoebe Cridland](https://www.olympics.com.au/olympians/phoebe-cridland/)
- **Current Training Base:** Recently relocated to Oslo, Norway, after being offered a position on an elite Norwegian club team, giving her access to top-tier training facilities and European competition circuits [OWIA — Phoebe Cridland](https://www.owia.org/phoebe-cridland.html)
- **Education:** Currently completing a double degree -- Bachelor of Science and Bachelor of Law at the University of Sydney. She began her studies in 2020 after graduating from SCEGGS Darlinghurst (a private girls' school in inner-Sydney). During her early university years, she resided at St John's College within the University of Sydney. She joined Sydney Uni Sport's Elite Athlete Program in 2020, balancing the demands of a double degree with international training blocks, European winters, and World Cup competition [Sydney Uni Sport — Cridland selected for 2026 Olympics](https://susf.com.au/phoebe-cridland-selected-for-milano-cortina-2026-winter-olympics/)
- **How She Started Skiing:** Cridland came to cross-country skiing through Interschools racing, initially competing for PLC Sydney before moving to SCEGGS. A background in gymnastics sharpened her balance and coordination on snow. She was just 15 when she first qualified for the Australian national team, and the following year she represented the Australian Junior Cross Country Ski Team in competitions in Canada and Europe [Sydney Uni Sport — Cridland selected for 2026 Olympics](https://susf.com.au/phoebe-cridland-selected-for-milano-cortina-2026-winter-olympics/) [OWIA — Phoebe Cridland](https://www.owia.org/phoebe-cridland.html)
- **Contributions Beyond Racing:** She served as athlete representative on Snow Australia's Cross Country Committee from 2022 to 2024, giving back to the sport administratively while still an active competitor [Snow Australia — New XC Athlete Reps](https://www.snow.org.au/news/new-xc-athlete-reps)
