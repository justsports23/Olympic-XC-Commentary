## Personal Life & Background
Linda Kaparkaleja hails from **Madona**, a town in the Vidzeme region of Latvia known for its cross-country skiing infrastructure and roller skiing traditions ([LSM.lv](https://www.lsm.lv/raksts/sports/ziemas-sports/27.12.2025-merktieciba-izaugsme-un-moderns-treninu-plans-slepotajs-kaparkalejs-gatavojas-olimpiskajai-debijai.a627614/)). She comes from a skiing family: her brother **Lauris Kaparkalejs** (born 13 December 2003) is also a Latvian cross-country and roller skier competing at these same Olympics, making them a sibling duo representing Latvia on the world stage ([Olympedia](http://www.olympedia.org/athletes/139053)). Lauris won the Junior World Championship title in roller ski sprint and finished second in a senior Roller Ski World Cup sprint, while Linda has excelled in women's junior roller skiing ([ProXCskiing](https://www.proxcskiing.com/traditional-skiing/linda-kaparkaleja-takes-junior-sprint-win-in-val-di-fiemme/)). Both siblings were named to Latvia's biggest-ever Winter Olympic team for Milano Cortina 2026, a source of immense pride for the Madona region ([LSM.lv — Biggest-ever Latvian team](https://eng.lsm.lv/article/culture/sport/20.01.2026-biggest-ever-latvian-winter-olympic-team-picked.a630669/)). Linda's path to the Olympics has been built primarily through roller skiing, where she dominated at the junior level, before transitioning to snow-based competition at the senior international level.
