## Key Storylines
- Karaliova is the sole Belarusian cross-country skier at the 2026 Olympics, competing under the AIN (Individual Neutral Athlete) designation after FIS cleared Belarusian athletes to return to competition in late 2025.
- She effectively missed the 2022 Olympics due to a COVID-19 positive test, making this her true Olympic debut despite being nominated for the Beijing Games.
- After years of suspension from international competition, she has spoken about the challenges of returning: "During the period of suspension, competition has grown stronger. Everyone is improving, but we are also making progress. We are gradually adapting to these conditions, to this snow."
- Her best result of the season (33rd at Val di Fiemme) shows she is capable of competing in the back end of the World Cup field on her best day.
- She has some familiarity with the Tesero course from the Tour de Ski stage held at Val di Fiemme in January 2026.
