## Personal Life & Background
Nopriienko is a deaf athlete who has been competing at the Deaflympics since 2015, a quadrennial international event reserved for people with a hearing threshold of less than 55 decibels and no hearing correction device ([Nordic Mag](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/2026-olympics-cross-country-skiing-yelizaveta-nopriienko-a-deaf-athlete-who-won-three-gold-medals-at-the-deaflympics-was-caught-during-the-skiathlon-0207/)). After dominating the Deaflympics -- winning three gold medals and a silver at the 2024 Winter Deaflympics in Erzurum, Turkey -- the 27-year-old set her sights on competing against able-bodied athletes at the highest level ([Nordic Mag](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/2026-olympics-cross-country-skiing-yelizaveta-nopriienko-a-deaf-athlete-who-won-three-gold-medals-at-the-deaflympics-was-caught-during-the-skiathlon-0207/)). She competed on the FIS World Cup circuit during the 2025-26 Olympic season, starting at Ruka (Finland), and ultimately qualified for the Milano Cortina 2026 Olympic Games ([Nordic Mag](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/2026-olympics-cross-country-skiing-yelizaveta-nopriienko-a-deaf-athlete-who-won-three-gold-medals-at-the-deaflympics-was-caught-during-the-skiathlon-0207/)). Her transition from Deaflympics dominance to mainstream Olympic competition makes her one of the most inspiring crossover stories at these Games.
