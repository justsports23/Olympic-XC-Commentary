# Venue History — Tesero / Val di Fiemme

Val di Fiemme (Fiemme Valley) in the Trentino region of northern Italy is one of the most storied venues in Nordic skiing history. The Cross Country and Biathlon Center Fabio Canal (formerly the Lago di Tesero Cross-Country Stadium), located in the village of Lago in the municipality of Tesero, has hosted over 400 international races. With the 2026 Winter Olympics, Val di Fiemme joins Oslo and Sapporo as one of the only locations to have hosted all major events of cross-country skiing: the Olympics, World Championships, and World Cup. ([Fiemme World Cup — History](https://www.fiemmeworldcup.com/en/organising-committee/the-history))

The venue sits at approximately 830m above sea level and features 19km of trails with highly varied terrain including steep climbs, tight bends, technical descents, and demanding restarts. Loop lengths range from 1.6 to 3.75 km. ([NBC Olympics — Tesero Venue Guide](https://www.nbcolympics.com/news/cross-country-skiing-101-venue))

## World Championships at Val di Fiemme

### 2013 FIS Nordic World Ski Championships
**Dates:** 20 February -- 3 March 2013 ([Wikipedia — FIS Nordic WSC 2013](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FIS_Nordic_World_Ski_Championships_2013))

The third time Val di Fiemme hosted the Nordic World Ski Championships. Norway dominated the women's cross-country events, with Marit Bjoergen winning four gold medals and Therese Johaug claiming three golds.

### 2003 FIS Nordic World Ski Championships
**Dates:** 18 February -- 1 March 2003 ([Wikipedia — FIS Nordic WSC 2003](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FIS_Nordic_World_Ski_Championships_2003))

The second time Val di Fiemme hosted the championships. Norway's Bente Skari dominated the individual distance events in what would be the final championships of her career. Estonia's Kristina Smigun was the other standout performer with one gold, two silvers, and one bronze.

| Pursuit / Skiathlon 5+5km | Kristina Smigun (EST) | [DATA NOT FOUND] | [DATA NOT FOUND] |

**Notable:** Bente Skari claimed gold in both the 10km classic and the 15km classic mass start, bringing her career tally of World Championship gold medals to five. She retired after the 2003 season as one of the most dominant classic skiers in history. ([Britannica — Bente Skari](https://www.britannica.com/biography/Bente-Skari)) ([Olympics.com — Bente Skari](https://www.olympics.com/en/athletes/bente-skari)) The 2003 championships introduced the skiathlon format for pursuit races.

### 1991 FIS Nordic World Ski Championships
**Dates:** 7--17 February 1991 ([Wikipedia — FIS Nordic WSC 1991](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FIS_Nordic_World_Ski_Championships_1991))

The first time Val di Fiemme hosted the championships and the first time the FIS Nordic World Ski Championships were held south of the Alps, in the Mediterranean basin. The Soviet Union's Elena Valbe was the dominant individual performer, winning three gold medals.

**Women's Cross-Country Events:**

**Notable:** Lyubov Yegorova's gold medals at Val di Fiemme 1991 made her the last athlete to win gold for the Soviet Union before its dissolution later that year. Elena Valbe won gold in the 10km, 15km, and relay, plus silver in the 30km -- one of the most dominant championship performances in history. ## Tour de Ski at Val di Fiemme

Val di Fiemme is the only venue to have hosted a stage in every edition of the Tour de Ski since its inception in 2006/07. The Tour concludes each year with its signature final stage: the climb up the Alpe Cermis alpine course in Cavalese, adjacent to the Tesero stadium. The stage covers 9.0 km in total, with the climb itself measuring 3.6 km at an average gradient of 11.6% and a maximum gradient of 28.0%. ([Tour de Ski — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tour_de_Ski))

## Other Major Events at Val di Fiemme

### FIS Cross-Country World Cup
Val di Fiemme has regularly hosted World Cup stages, most often in conjunction with the Tour de Ski. The first FIS World Cup event in Val di Fiemme took place in 1990, one year before the inaugural World Championships. In addition to Tour de Ski stages, the venue has hosted standalone World Cup distance events. ([Fiemme World Cup — History](https://www.fiemmeworldcup.com/en/organising-committee/the-history))

### 2026 Milano Cortina Winter Olympics
Tesero Cross-Country Skiing Stadium in Val di Fiemme hosts cross-country skiing and Nordic combined for the 2026 Winter Olympics and Paralympics. The Olympic women's skiathlon (10km+10km) was held on February 8, 2026, with Frida Karlsson (SWE) winning gold (53:45.2), Ebba Andersson (SWE) silver (+51.0), and Heidi Weng (NOR) bronze. The women's sprint was held on February 11, 2026. The women's 10km interval start free is scheduled for February 12, 2026. ([Olympics.com — Tesero](https://milanocortina2026.olympics.com/en/tesero)) ([Nordic Mag — Tesero Stadium Guide](https://www.nordicmag.info/en/2026-olympics-tesero-cross-country-skiing-stadium-everything-you-need-to-know-about-the-val-di-fiemme-stadium-which-will-host-the-cross-country-skiing-events-at-the-milan-cortina-2026-olympics-0103/))

### Marcialonga
The famous Marcialonga long-distance cross-country ski marathon (70km, classic technique) passes through Val di Fiemme and Val di Fassa annually, starting in Moena and finishing in Cavalese. It is one of the most prestigious events in the Worldloppet circuit. The course passes near the Tesero stadium area.
